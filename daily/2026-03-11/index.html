Morning Brief

At market close, US stocks slipped modestly — the S&P 500 ended 6,781.48 (-0.21%), while the Nasdaq finished 22,697.10 (+0.01%). On the safe-haven front, Gold fell 0.58% to $5,199.20, the 10-year Treasury yield held flat at 4.14%, and the dollar strengthened (EUR/USD 1.1608). WTI crude rose 2.09% to $85.19/bbl.

What This Means

Stocks pulled back modestly yesterday. The S&P 500 closed 6,781.48 (-0.21%), the Nasdaq 22,697.10 (+0.01%), the Dow 47,706.51 (-0.07%), the Russell 2000 2,548.08 (-0.22%). Notably, 1 of the other major indices held positive — this looks more like rotation than broad risk-off selling. On the cross-asset front: Gold fell 0.58% to $5,199.20, the 10-year Treasury yield held flat at 4.14%, WTI crude rose 2.09% to $85.19/bbl, and the dollar strengthened 0.12%.

Geopolitics was the session's dominant force. Three cargo ships struck off Iran's coast, UK says, including one in Strait of Hormuz. The Strait of Hormuz is the world's most important oil chokepoint — roughly 20% of global oil supply transits through it daily. Any credible disruption threat moves energy markets and ripples through inflation expectations, transportation costs, and emerging-market currencies. A notable cross-asset signal: gold fell 0.58% despite the equity decline. Normally, geopolitical stress pushes investors into gold. When gold drops alongside stocks, it typically signals that the dollar is the real safe-haven destination — investors are buying USD, not bullion. The 10-year yield's near-flat move confirms there was no meaningful rotation into Treasuries either — the flight-to-safety trade went straight to cash and the dollar.

For diversified ETF holders, here's what to watch: Yesterday's session was quiet — no major allocation changes are warranted. Modest moves without a clear catalyst are noise, not signal.

Keep a close eye on: geopolitical headlines — any escalation or de-escalation will move oil, FX, and risk sentiment quickly; gold — if risk-off sentiment re-intensifies, gold could recover sharply as the safe-haven trade catches up.

Pre-market is pointing to a positive open (S&P +0.23% | Nasdaq +0.20% | Dow +0.24%). The market is treating yesterday's decline as a dip, not a trend. Watch whether buyers follow through with conviction once cash markets open, or whether early strength fades.

APAC was mixed overnight (3 up, 2 down) — Nikkei 225 led (+1.43%), while Nifty 50 lagged (-1.63%).

CPI is today's key risk event at 8:30 AM ET. A hot print pushes yields higher, pressures growth stocks (QQQ), and pushes out the Fed's rate-cut timeline — defensives (XLU, XLP) and TIPS would benefit. A cool print revives rate-cut hopes and lifts long bonds (TLT) and tech. Either way, be positioned before the release, not after.
