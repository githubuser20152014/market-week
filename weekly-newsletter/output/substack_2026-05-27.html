Micron Hit $1 Trillion. Bonds Said No.

The Morning Brief - Market intelligence at the open

AI euphoria and geopolitical chaos are trading at the same altitude right now, and the market is running both trades simultaneously. The dominant signal is a sharp split: equities at all-time highs while oil craters 3.8% on Iran peace deal optimism. Futures are pushing higher pre-open, but the bond market is not confirming the party - 10Y notes slipping again overnight. One of them is lying.

Yesterday's US session delivered a fractured rally. The S&P 500 closed at 7,519, up 0.61%, and the Nasdaq surged 1.19% to 26,656, both printing all-time highs. The Russell 2000 led the tape, up 1.79% to 2,920, as small-caps caught a rotation bid. The Dow lagged, off 0.23% at 50,461, weighed by energy names. The story underneath is the real tell: WTI crude collapsed 3.82% to $90.30, gold slipped 0.45% to $4,480, and the 10Y yield fell 6.5 basis points to 4.493% as the Iran-deal narrative pulled a safe-haven bid out of Treasuries and dumped it back into risk.

The causal chain runs through the Strait of Hormuz. U.S. CENTCOM strikes on Iranian missile sites, framed as "self-defense" to reopen the strait, triggered a crude selloff because markets are pricing a 30-day reopening timeline. At the same time, Micron's 19.3% leap to a $1 trillion market cap on AI memory demand pulled Nasdaq leadership sharply higher, with Taiwan chip stocks adding fuel after Nvidia announced $150 billion in new spending plans. New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh, who officially took the helm this week, is facing an immediate inflation test: oil moves of this magnitude, driven by a still-unresolved geopolitical blockade, are exactly the kind of supply shock that complicates a rate path. Markets are already pricing a potential 2027 hike.

Here's my read:

The fractured rally exposes the real tension in this market. Equities are celebrating AI capex and small-cap rotation on geopolitical risk-off. Bonds are saying no thanks - a 6.5 basis point rally is Iran optimism, not a fundamental rate pivot. The Warsh Fed faces an energy supply shock with 2027 hikes priced in. That's a headwind for duration, and it's why long bonds are selling even as stocks rally. The Russell 2000 outperforming by 1.2 percentage points signals real rotation out of defensive mega-cap into cyclicals. That trade has legs if rates stay stable or fall further. It dies fast if the 10Y yield reverses back above 4.55%. Pre-market futures are firm (S&P +0.32%, Nasdaq +0.51%, Dow +0.46%), but no major data is scheduled today - making the Iran negotiation headline the single biggest swing factor. A credible Hormuz reopening announcement hammers XLE further and compresses the inflationary premium in rates. A breakdown in Doha talks reverses the crude selloff instantly and reprices everything. Watch crude at the $90 level as the session's fulcrum.
