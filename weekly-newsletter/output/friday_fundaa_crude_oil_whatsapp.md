Hot take: "oil prices" isn't a thing. What the Brent is going on here? 🤬

There are *two* oil prices. The media picks one without telling you which one. You've been nodding along to headlines for years not knowing this 🙃

*WTI* = American oil. Priced in Oklahoma. Landlocked. Unbothered. Doesn't care about the Middle East.

*Brent* = Global oil. Shipped by tanker. Very dramatic. Extremely aware of the Middle East.

Normally Brent costs ~$5 more than WTI. Fine. Shipping fees. Whatever.

But right now the Strait of Hormuz — the world's most important oil pipe — is basically shut. One in five barrels of global oil goes through it daily. US/Israeli strikes on Iran. You know how it is.

Brent absolutely lost its mind. Went from the mid-$60s to over $100.

WTI watched from Oklahoma like 😐

Here's the fun part: underneath all this drama, the actual oil market is *oversupplied*. Goldman, JPMorgan, and the EIA all think Brent should be around $60 for the year. The spike is pure fear premium — $10-15 a barrel of "oh no, what if Hormuz stays closed."

Which means every time you wince at the pump, part of what you're paying for is geopolitical anxiety over a waterway most people couldn't find on a map.
