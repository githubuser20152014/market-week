Stocks Booked Their Best First Half Since 2020. The Yen Hit a 40-Year Low Doing It.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

The S&P 500 and Nasdaq just closed their best first half since 2020. Congratulations to whoever owns five stocks. Everyone else got the rally as background noise while a currency quietly broke.

The yen crossed 162.40 to the dollar overnight, a 40-year low, after Japan spent $74 billion defending it and lost anyway. Gold sold off into the equity party, and U.S.-Iran talks - the ones that were supposedly settled - cracked again per CNBC, less than two weeks after the market priced in peace.

None of this shows up in the index level. All of it shows up in what happens next.

Here's my read:

The S&P 500 added 0.8% to 7,499.36 and the Nasdaq ripped 1.5% to 26,213.72, the strongest first half since 2020 - if you squint past the fact that five stocks did the heavy lifting. The Dow limped to +0.26% at 52,319, the Russell 2000 crawled 0.46% to 3,024, and small caps still haven't been invited to this party. Gold fell 0.84% to $3,989, oil dropped 1.18% to $68.68, the 10-year eased 2 bps to 4.372%, and the dollar index sat flat at 101.34.

Two forces built the H1 close: an AI and Magnificent Seven rebound after June's wobble, and the Strait of Hormuz stand-down that let everyone buy stocks again. Oil's slide is that de-escalation premium draining out, not demand falling apart. Gold selling off into a risk-on tape fits the same script, since safe-haven money leaves once the scary headline disappears. Except the scary headline didn't disappear. CNBC reported a breakdown in U.S.-Iran talks overnight, and the yen crossed 162.40 after Japan burned $74 billion defending it. That's not a rounding error. That's a currency regime cracking while the market debates chip stocks.

The One Trade: Long $XLE

U.S.-Iran talks are showing cracks per CNBC this morning, and oil at $68.68 has already stripped out the Hormuz risk premium that was built in just two weeks ago. Bad diplomatic news reprices energy faster than any other sector right now, and that's the asymmetry.

Confirms: XLE holds above $88 through the first 30 minutes of trading and WTI crude reclaims $69.50 on any negative Iran headline out of Doha before noon.

Kill switch: A joint Doha briefing confirming ceasefire progress sends WTI back below $67 and invalidates the trade. Get out fast.
