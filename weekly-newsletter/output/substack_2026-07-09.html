The Ceasefire Died Overnight. Gold Hit $4,113 Before Breakfast.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

The Iran ceasefire is dead, and gold didn't wait for a press conference to notice. Gold surged 1.03% to $4,113 yesterday while the Dow dropped 1.09% to 52,348, because when the Strait of Hormuz turns into a live threat, the market's honesty detector only has one setting.

Three things happened at once. Trump declared the ceasefire over after attacks on commercial vessels near the Strait of Hormuz, prompting US airstrikes on more than 80 Iranian targets. New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh ran his first FOMC minutes into a split committee with no consensus on cuts, right as June inflation expectations climbed to 3.7%. And PepsiCo missed earnings, confirming the American consumer is tightening its belt at the worst possible moment for a Fed that can't afford to ease.

Nasdaq futures are up 0.57% pre-market anyway. Tech thinks none of this applies to it.

Here's my read:

The dollar index bled to 100.97 despite a hawkish set of Fed minutes, which is the tell: rate hawkishness usually buys the dollar a bid, and this time it bought nothing. Small caps and dividend stocks took the brunt of the rate-sensitivity repricing, the Russell 2000 falling 0.88% to 2,956 while the Nasdaq eked out a 0.20% gain, a divergence that only makes sense if you assume megacap tech is exempting itself from a story it should be part of. Capital is leaving cyclicals and small caps and it isn't coming back until the geopolitical risk clears or Warsh's committee finds a spine. Today's open is the test of whether tech's exemption actually holds.

The One Trade: Long $GLD

Gold at $4,113 is absorbing three independent bid sources at once: the Hormuz ceasefire collapse, a dollar that can't rally on Fed hawkishness, and a new Fed chair whose minutes show zero consensus on easing. That convergence rarely unwinds in a single session.

Confirms: GLD holds above $408 through the first 30 minutes and doesn't give back the overnight futures gain as equities open higher.
Kill switch: A credible ceasefire signal or Hormuz reopening headline sends WTI down more than 3% intraday and the dollar index back above 101.50. The geopolitical premium collapses. Get out fast.

What else I'm watching

  • Stay long GLD, or add on any dip below $4,100: three tailwinds hitting one asset at once, and yesterday's gain into a flat dollar confirms buyers are in control, not chasing momentum.
  • Trim or avoid IWM into the open: the rate-sensitivity divergence against Nasdaq is textbook, and PepsiCo's consumer miss gives it a fundamental backstop. Don't you dare hold this through a yield spike past 4.60%.
  • ITA is a structural hold with a tactical add: the NATO Ankara summit is converting 5% GDP spending pledges into actual procurement contracts, and the Iran escalation just accelerated the timeline.
  • XLE deserves a starter position, not a full allocation: WTI at $73.77 hasn't priced a full Hormuz closure yet. If shipping disruption headlines escalate, this becomes the momentum trade fast.
  • Stay away from TLT: a split Fed, 3.7% inflation expectations, and an active Hormuz conflict are three separate reasons yields keep climbing. Long duration is the wrong side of this trade this week.
