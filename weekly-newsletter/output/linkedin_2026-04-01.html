The One Trade: Long $GLD

Trump signals a U.S. exit from the Iran conflict. Markets repriced the entire geopolitical risk premium in a single session: Nasdaq +3.83%, S&P 500 +2.91%, Russell 2000 +3.40%. APAC confirmed the euphoria — KOSPI up 7.5%, Nikkei up 3.8%.

This is the kind of move that clears out safe-haven positions. Traders dump gold, sell bonds, rotate into risk.

But gold barely budged. +0.004%.

Here's why this matters:

Gold refusing to sell off on a 3%+ equity rally tells you the smart money isn't convinced the geopolitical all-clear is real. The 10Y only dipped 4.6bps. WTI is still seesawing around $100. Neither asset collapsed the way they should if the peace trade were real.

That divergence is a warning flag. Either inflation/dollar concerns are still present, or traders think this Iran exit narrative will reverse. Either way, the structural bid under gold didn't break.

The setup is simple: if gold holds above yesterday's close through 11am ET with no meaningful decline despite continued equity strength, that's your confirmation the bid is structural. The risk is a confirmed, concrete U.S.-Iran ceasefire or a sharp USD reversal — either would invalidate the thesis.
