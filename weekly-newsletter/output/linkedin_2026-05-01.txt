Markets rallied +2% on GDP yesterday. Gold sold off into it.

That's the tension worth understanding.

Q1 GDP came in at +2.0% versus a prior +0.5% — a sharp rebound that undercut the recession narrative. Apple guided above estimates. Traders bought the growth story and called it a day. But three other markets pushed back: gold dropped 0.7%, the yen surged 2.25%, and the 10-year Treasury barely moved despite the growth beat. When bonds, gold, and FX all disagree with equities on the same session, it's worth asking why.

The answer: PCE is still running at 3.5% YoY. The Strait of Hormuz is on Day 63 of a blockade. The War Powers 60-day deadline just expired with the Trump administration claiming a ceasefire exemption Congress can challenge at any moment. WTI is holding at $105.

Institutional players used the GDP rally to book profits on the geopolitical bid. The macro case hasn't changed — the session did.
