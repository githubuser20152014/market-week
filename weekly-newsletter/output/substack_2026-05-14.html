Jensen Huang Joined the State Department. Nasdaq Gained 1.2%. Gold Stayed at $4,702 Anyway.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

The Trump-Xi summit in Beijing is the only thing that matters right now, and the market has decided that Nvidia is the new primary instrument of American diplomacy. While the diplomats are arguing over territory, Jensen Huang is busy unlocking revenue. The Nasdaq jumped 1.2% after reports that the U.S. cleared H200 chip sales to China - a massive revenue unlock that proves the AI supercycle isn't just a tech trend; it's a pillar of statecraft.

But while the tech bros are high on chip-fumes, the bond market is looking for the nearest exit. Gold is still squatting stubbornly above $4,700, and the Strait of Hormuz remains a physical choke point for the global economy. It’s a classic case of cognitive dissonance: the equity market is pricing in a diplomatic miracle, while the commodities floor is pricing in a permanent state of siege.

The smart money isn't selling its insurance just because there’s a new export license on the table. In fact, the smart money is staying firmly in gold.

Here's my read:

The presence of Jensen Huang in Beijing alongside President Trump is the clearest signal yet of the "Peace through Chips" doctrine. By clearing the H200 sales, the administration is effectively using Nvidia as a carrot to keep the summit from devolving into a shouting match over Taiwan. This is a direct catalyst for the semiconductor complex, particularly SOXX and SMH, which have been starving for a structural revenue catalyst that isn't just "more data centers in Omaha."

However, the macro backdrop is getting weirder. Kevin Warsh's Senate confirmation as Fed Chair introduces a massive wildcard. The markets are desperately trying to price the "Warsh Era" as potentially more dovish in response to the energy shock. It's a bold strategy: the bond market is being asked to believe the Fed can ignore April CPI at 3.8% and PPI at 6% simply because the Strait of Hormuz is closed. It’s like trying to put out a forest fire with a spreadsheet—technically possible in a simulation, but the real-world results usually involve a lot of smoke. This is why the 10-year yield is refusing to dive even as tech rips; nobody wants to be the first one to call "all clear" on inflation.

The One Trade: LONG $SOXX

The U.S. clearing Nvidia H200 sales to China is a named export license catalyst, not a rumor. Jensen Huang is physically in Beijing at Trump's request, making a formal tech-trade framework the highest-probability summit outcome today. If SOXX holds yesterday's levels and the QQQ stays above the 9:45 AM check, the momentum is real. You are trading the diplomacy, not the earnings. Kill switch: Xi issues a formal Taiwan warning that derails the summit. If the geopolitical red lines start getting drawn in ink, get out fast.

What else I'm watching

  • Gold is the truth-teller: At $4,702, gold (GLD) is refusing to sell off into the tech rip. That is the tell. The geopolitical premium isn't leaving the room, and you shouldn't dare sell your defensive core while the Strait of Hormuz is still a live wire.
  • Energy is a structural hold: Stay with XLE. With the IEA flagging depleting inventories and WTI at $101, the energy trade is structurally supported. The only risk is a surprise ceasefire headline from Beijing that gaps oil down 5% intraday, but until then, the supply-side reality is the boss.
  • The Dow 50,000 wall: The DIA is still gasping for air below 50,000. Underweight Dow-heavy exposure and avoid broad industrials like XLI. The underperformance versus the Nasdaq is a regime signal, not noise. The industrial complex is choking on high rates, and Jensen Huang's chip deal doesn't fix a margin squeeze.
  • Watch TLT for the Warsh signal: If bond prices firm today despite equity strength, the market is officially pricing Warsh as a dovish pivot candidate against the inflation backdrop. That would be the green light to add duration. If TLT continues to slide, the stagflation read wins and cash or short-duration (SGOV, BIL) remains the only place to hide.
