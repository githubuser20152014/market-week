Oil Crashed 4%. SpaceX Hit $2.1 Trillion. Gold Believed Neither.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

The US and Iran signed a peace deal. Oil crashed 4%. The S&P hit a fresh all-time high at 7,554.29, the Dow set a record at 51,671.03, and the Nasdaq ripped 3.07% to 26,683.94, dragged there by a rocket company that still doesn't turn a profit.

Everyone except gold is celebrating. Spot gold held firm at $4,362.30, refusing to give back a single dollar while the rest of the market threw a party. That's not a rounding error. That's a market that read the same peace deal and decided to keep its coat on.

One asset class believed the ceasefire. One didn't. Guess which one has been right more often.

Here's my read:

The dual engines today were the Geneva Accord on the Strait of Hormuz and the SpaceX afterburn. Trump signed a preliminary peace deal with Iran to end three months of conflict, with the strait reopening by Friday, which functioned like pulling the emergency brake on energy-driven inflation. WTI crude falling nearly 4% in a single session is the market pricing a disinflationary dividend: cheaper energy now, lower CPI later, and a Fed that finally gets cover to ease. Meanwhile SpaceX surged another 19.6% to a $2.1 trillion valuation in day two of trading, reigniting IPO momentum and pulling growth tech along for the ride.

But gold above $4,300 while oil drops sharply is the tell nobody priced correctly. Gold is not just an inflation hedge here, it's absorbing the residual geopolitical risk that the equity market is choosing to ignore: a 60-day nuclear negotiation window that hasn't closed, and Israel's active military posture in Lebanon that hasn't changed. Gold has trust issues, and frankly, who can blame it.

The One Trade: LONG $GLD

Gold refusing to sell off into a massive oil-driven risk-on rally signals buyers are defending the floor against the unresolved 60-day Iran nuclear clock and Israel's active military posture in Lebanon.

Confirms: GLD holds above $423 (spot near $4,320) through 11:00 AM ET as the broad equity gap-up stabilizes. If gold doesn't give up ground while the S&P rallies, the safe-haven bid is structural, not stale.

Kill switch: Spot gold breaks $4,280 on a confirmed Hormuz transit report Friday that removes all residual conflict premium, or Netanyahu signals an unexpected withdrawal from Lebanon, collapsing the geopolitical bid entirely. If that happens, get out fast.

What else I'm watching

  • Energy ETFs ($XLE, $XOP): trim into any early bounce. The Hormuz reopening removes three months of conflict premium from crude in one session, and the Strategic Petroleum Reserve sitting at 1983 lows confirms this deal landed at exactly the wrong inventory moment to cushion the fall. Stay away from chasing a relief rally here.
  • Tech ($QQQ): the SpaceX IPO is functioning as a liquidity multiplier for the entire AI/tech complex, and the trade nobody is talking about is the OpenAI and Anthropic IPO pipeline riding the same wave. Add on any open fade if Nasdaq futures hold above 30,800.
  • Long bonds ($TLT): the oil collapse is disinflationary, which gives Kevin Warsh's Fed room to lean dovish. Watch the 10Y for a pullback toward 4.40% as the tell that the bond market is starting to price it in.
  • South Korean defense: KOSPI popped 2.11% on post-Iran-war defense sales speculation, the irony of a peace deal fueling a weapons stock rally apparently lost on no one but the market itself. Don't you dare chase it. With the Geneva Accord in place, that's a one-day sentiment pop, not a new structural theme.
