The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

Ninety U.S. airstrikes hit Iran overnight. Iran fired back at Kuwait and Bahrain. Tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz ground to a near-standstill. Gold, the asset that's supposed to panic first and ask questions later, dropped half a percent.

The chip stock that should be carrying the market is instead the reason futures are red.

Here's my read:

The S&P closed up 0.42% to 7,575.39 yesterday like nothing was wrong. The people paid to panic aren't panicking. They're hedging.

The One Trade: Long $XLE

Confirms: XLE holds above $105 through the first 30 minutes of trading and WTI stays above $72.50.
