The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

CPI day. The 8:30 ET print either confirms inflation is dead or revives it at the worst possible moment.

The Cleveland Fed Nowcast is at 4.18%. That's the number. Just above the threshold that kills every remaining rate cut and turns Kevin Warsh's June 17 FOMC debut into a hawkish obituary for 2026. S&P futures are down 0.77% and Nasdaq futures are off 1.23% before the open. The market is pricing in the bad print before it happens.

Classic.

Here's my read:

Yesterday's session was a quiet schizophrenia. Nasdaq closed up 0.86% at 25,929. S&P 500 added 0.30% to 7,405. Dow shed 0.16%, Russell 2000 tagged along at +0.77%. Then Apple unveiled the big Siri AI update everyone waited years for, and the stock slid. Gold dropped 1.60% to $4,191.80 despite a weaker dollar. The crisis hedge sold off on the same day the U.S. bombed Iran. The 10Y yield ticked up 1.6 bps to 4.552%. WTI crude slid 0.67% to $87.61. USD Index broke under the 100 handle to 99.91.

The Hormuz situation has not improved. An Apache helicopter was attacked near the Strait. The U.S. responded with overnight strikes on Iranian targets. Energy Secretary Wright is publicly flagging rising traffic through the chokepoint. Oil is "choppy" at $87.55, which is the market's polite word for no idea what happens next. A rotation out of AI semis (Micron, Nvidia) into banks and pharma started Tuesday. Today's CPI is the scorekeeper: 4.2% YoY is the line in the sand. Above it and Warsh turns hawkish on June 17. Below 4.0% and the dip-buyers get their day.

One of those outcomes is priced in. The other is not.

The One Trade: Short $TLT

CPI hits at 8:30 with the Cleveland Fed Nowcast at 4.18% and the 10Y already sitting at 4.552%. A hot print above 4.2% removes every remaining rate cut from the 2026 calendar and sends bond prices lower with no policy backstop in sight.

Confirms: TLT trading below $88.50 by 9:15 AM ET after the CPI release, with the 10Y yield moving above 4.60% on the print.

Kill switch: CPI prints below 4.0% YoY. At that level, rate cut expectations revive, TLT rallies, and this thesis is off. Get out fast.
