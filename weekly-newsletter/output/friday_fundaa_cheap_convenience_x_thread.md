1/ The era of the $15 burrito delivery is dead. 🌯💀

If you feel like your apps are suddenly death-by-a-thousand-cuts, you're right. The $553 "Convenience Tax" has arrived. 

Here’s why the era of cheap apps is over. 🧵

2/ For a decade, we lived in a bubble. 

Uber, DoorDash, and budget airlines were subsidized by two things: 
⛽️ Sub-$70 oil. 
💸 Zero interest rates (cheap VC cash). 

Both just evaporated. 

3/ With WTI crude near $100/bbl (thanks, Strait of Hormuz), the "input cost" for moving a human or a taco has exploded. 

These companies have razor-thin margins. They can't absorb a 40% fuel spike. So they pass it to you in the "plumbing" of the receipt. 🧾

4/ The Breakdown: 

🚗 Lyft/Uber: +$0.55 fuel surcharge per ride. 
🥡 DoorDash/UberEats: +$0.45-$2.00 in new fees. 
⛽️ Gas Pump: +$0.90/gallon on average since last year. 
✈️ Airlines: $20-$50 fuel surcharges are back. 

5/ Add it up for the average household? 

That's **$553 per year** just to stay as "convenient" as you were 12 months ago. 

It’s not just "inflation." It’s a structural shift in how services are priced when energy isn't cheap anymore. 

6/ The 2010s were the anomaly. 

We had a "Convenience Subsidy" where tech companies lost money to win your loyalty. That subsidy is gone. 

"Instant" and "delivered" are becoming luxury services again, not defaults. 

7/ The next time you see a "temporary surcharge," don't expect it to leave. 

It’s just the market finally charging you what the delivery actually costs.
