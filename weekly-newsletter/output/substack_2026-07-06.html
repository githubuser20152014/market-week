The Jobs Report Missed by 58,000. The Dow Hit a Record Anyway.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

Nonfarm payrolls came in at 57,000, less than half of the 115,000 economists expected, a miss so clean it deserves an apology letter. Wall Street's response was to throw a party anyway. The Dow closed at a record 52,900, up 1.1%, because in this market bad news for the economy is good news for rate cuts, and rate cuts are all anyone wants to hear about.

Not everyone bought the story. Gold ripped 1.25% to $4,165 even as the dollar firmed, a combination that isn't supposed to coexist and did anyway. The Nasdaq fell 0.8%, the Russell 2000 dropped 0.55%, and small caps, the part of the market that actually needs the economy to function, sat this rally out entirely.

Half the market partied. The other half read the fine print.

Here's my read:

Blame June's payrolls: 57,000 jobs against a 115,000 forecast, the kind of gap that turns "soft landing" into a phrase people stop saying with a straight face. Dow blue chips rallied on the "bad news is good news" reflex while small caps, which need real growth and not just Fed optionality, got left holding the bag. Tech sold off on plain profit-taking after a hot run, with China tech-restriction headlines giving traders a second reason to sell first and ask questions later. The real tell is gold's 1.25% surge to $4,165 despite a firmer dollar: that isn't a rate trade, that's China testing an ICBM into the South Pacific, fresh China-Russia naval drills near Qingdao, and a cargo ship getting hit in the Red Sea, all landing in the same 24 hours. Central banks are buying the fear, not the Fed pivot.

The One Trade: Long $GLD

Gold is holding above $4,150 despite a firmer dollar and a risk-on futures open, a refusal to sell that says institutional buyers are defending the level on both the rate-cut and geopolitical channels at once.

Confirms: GLD holds above the equivalent of 4,150 spot through 10:00 AM ET once the post-open dust settles, bonus points if TLT catches a bid after the 9:15 AM Industrial Production print.
Kill switch: GLD breaks below 4,120 spot intraday. That's the safe-haven bid unwinding and dollar strength taking over. Exit fast.

What else I'm watching

  • Hold or add to GLD on any intraday dip below 4,150. The dollar uptick hasn't dented it, and that's the tell, this is not just a rate trade.
  • Lean long TLT into the 9:15 AM Industrial Production release. A soft print extends the Fed-cut narrative; a beat and you cut the position fast, no arguments.
  • Stay away from chasing QQQ on the pre-market pop. A 1% Nasdaq futures bounce on no new fundamental catalyst is a relief trade, not a breakout, wait for the first 30 minutes to confirm.
  • Stay underweight IWM. Small caps need real economic growth, not rate-cut hope, and the Russell needs a clean break above 3,000 before that thesis changes.
  • The trade nobody is talking about: ITA. China's ICBM test and NATO 3.0 defense-spending headlines are structural tailwinds, and the Lockheed Martin bid for Ultra Maritime adds a near-term catalyst on top.
