# LinkedIn Post — Global Investor Edition 2026-04-04

**Why Europe Wins (and Japan Drowns) in This War**

On April 2, Trump announced the US would strike Iranian energy infrastructure if no deal is reached. Immediately, Brent crude surged 6%. By week-end, WTI hit $111.69 — up 69% year-to-date.

But here's what the markets are really saying:

The Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed. This is a structural supply shock, not a temporary spike.

**Geography now determines outcome:**

🟢 **Europe:** FTSE +4.7%, DAX +3.89%, Euro Stoxx +4.1%
   Why? War drives defense budgets higher (multi-decade order books for Rheinmetall, MBDA). Energy companies (Shell, BP, TotalEnergies) are capturing the oil premium directly in earnings.

🔴 **Japan & Australia:** Nikkei −0.4%, AUD −2.1%
   Why? Japan imports 90% of its crude. Triple-digit oil = direct margin squeeze on manufacturing, power generation, automotive. Australia's growth outlook darkens with China's slowdown.

**The Volatility Setup for Monday:**

The bond market fell 9.5 bps on the 10Y this week despite $111 oil. It was pricing stagflation (high prices + slow growth). Then March NFP printed Friday: **178,000 jobs added** vs. 65,000 expected.

That's not stagflation. That's **inflation in a growing economy** — and yields will reprice sharply higher when US markets reopen Monday.

**For investors:** The current positioning (Europe rally, US stable, Asia weak, oil $105-115) is crowded and fragile. One Trump escalation or ceasefire announcement could unwind the entire trade.

**Our one trade:** Energy (XLE). As long as the war continues and the Strait remains closed, crude supply is constrained and the risk premium is structural, not cyclical.
