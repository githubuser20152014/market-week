Gold Held $4,062 While the Nasdaq Lost 2.15%. Read the Room.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

Alphabet and Tesla torched their own earnings calls on AI capex fear. The White House slapped tariffs on 60 trade partners. Red Sea tankers took fire for a twelfth straight night, and Brent briefly touched $102. Futures this morning are up anyway. S&P futures +0.27% to 7,465. Nasdaq futures +0.25%. Apparently three separate reasons to panic add up to one green pre-market.

Gold isn't buying it. Gold held $4,062, up 0.38%, even as the Nasdaq cratered 2.15% to 25,137 and the 10-year yield climbed to 4.703%. Equities down, yields up, dollar flat, and gold still found a bid - that's not a rounding error, that's the bond market quietly pricing stagflation while equities pretend it's a buying dip. WTI dropped 3.46% to $89 despite the Brent spike, which means even oil traders don't trust the fear premium to stick.

Somebody in this market is wrong, and it's probably not the guy holding gold.

Here's my read:

The Alphabet and Tesla selloff isn't really about Alphabet and Tesla. Both companies confirmed the market's worst fear on AI capex: spending is accelerating while the return on it stays theoretical. Picture a developer pouring concrete on a skyscraper with no signed leases, then promising investors leasing season starts next quarter. Every cloud hyperscaler just got repriced off Alphabet's guidance, and Microsoft and Amazon haven't even reported yet.

Layer 10%-12.5% tariffs on 60 trade partners on top of Red Sea tanker strikes now in their twelfth consecutive night, and you get the 10-year's real message: the Fed does not cut into $100 oil and fresh import tariffs at the same time. Bonds never bought the equity rally of the past few weeks, and yesterday's 4.6 basis point jump is bonds saying it louder. KOSPI's 5.7% overnight collapse is the region getting that memo first, sitting at the intersection of tariff exposure and tech export dependence.

The One Trade: Long $GLD

Gold held $4,062 while equities fell 1.2%, yields rose 4.6 basis points, and the dollar barely moved: three headwinds that normally crush gold, and buyers absorbed all three.

Confirms: GLD stays above $390 intraday and extends toward $395 by 11:00 AM ET, especially if New Home Sales miss at 10:00 AM and reinforce the stagflation read.
Kill switch: A decisive S&P 500 move above 7,500 on a genuine de-escalation headline out of the Red Sea rotates capital out of safe havens and breaks GLD back below $388. Get out fast.

What else I'm watching

  • Hold GLD, don't you dare sell it here. Gold held its bid through a Nasdaq rout and a yield spike, the kind of divergence that only shows up when institutions are actually buying the fear, not renting it. Stay long unless New Home Sales beat big and the S&P clears 7,500 clean.
  • XLE is a coin flip dressed up as a trade. $89 WTI with Brent flirting with $102 should be bullish for producers, but yesterday's pullback off the highs says the market is already pricing demand destruction from tariffs. Add above $91, cut below $87, don't sit in the middle.
  • Stay away from QQQ until Microsoft and Amazon report. Alphabet just reset the entire hyperscaler cohort's capex math. Any pre-open Nasdaq bounce is a gift for trimming, not a green light to buy the dip.
  • ITA is the trade nobody's talking about. Twelve straight nights of Red Sea strikes, a NATO meeting in Brussels Monday, and zero progress at the Manila ASEAN forum add up to a multi-week defense revenue story hiding in plain sight.
  • TLT is not a rally to chase. A 4.703% 10-year reflects tariff-driven inflation repricing, not a one-day blip. Sit out duration until yields stabilize above 4.75% and actually reverse.
