The AI Rally Was Historic. KOSPI Down 10%, Nikkei Down 3.5%, Nasdaq Futures Down 2.8%.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

Washington gave Tehran an oil windfall and Trump immediately questioned whether Tehran would spend it on guns. The Swiss negotiators are on draft five. No one has signed anything. This is called constructive progress in diplomatic circles; in markets it is called a risk floor with no ceiling.

In Seoul, the KOSPI triggered a circuit breaker at minus 8%, halted for 20 minutes, and closed down 10% anyway. SK Hynix fell 12%. Samsung Electronics fell 7.5%. Foreign investors pulled $2.6 billion before lunch. The Nikkei fell 3.5%. The reason: the semiconductor AI rally that drove both indices to record highs ran out of buyers. Circuit breakers do not fix that. They delay it.

Nasdaq futures are pointing down 2.8% into Monday's open. The AI rally was historic. The invoice arrives this morning.

Here's my read:

Friday's US close set up the conflict clearly. The Russell 2000 gained 0.83% to 3,004 while the Nasdaq dropped 1.32% to 26,166: the tape split cleanly along duration. Domestic cyclicals don't care about Iranian oil waivers or Swiss peace drafts. Long-duration growth bets do. The S&P 500 slipped 0.37% to 7,472. Gold fell 1% to $4,140. WTI crude slid 1.36% to $73.80. The 10Y yield dipped just 1.2 bps to 4.451%: the bond market barely moved on a full risk-off day, which is the Fed's hawkish hold translating into market language. Higher-for-longer is not a slogan. It is a discount rate. Every uncut basis point is a tax on Nasdaq multiples. The Iran oil sanctions waiver adds real supply to crude; WTI fell anyway. But Trump's Hormuz warning puts a geopolitical floor under oil that prevents a clean short. Two forces. Neither wins cleanly. The Seoul semiconductor collapse is the cleanest signal: when the AI chip rally runs out of buyers, it doesn't drift lower. It gaps.

The One Trade: Short $QQQ

Nasdaq futures are off 2.8% pre-market, the KOSPI just tripped a circuit breaker and closed down 10%, and the Fed's rate-hike signal is still repricing growth multiples. The sellers have a morning head start.

Confirms: QQQ trades below $480 by 10:30 AM ET with no recovery bid, confirming sellers are in control through the first hour.

Kill switch: S&P futures recover above 7,450 before the open, signaling a gap-fill reversal; exit immediately if QQQ opens and holds above $521. Get out fast.
