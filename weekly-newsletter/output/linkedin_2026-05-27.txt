Nasdaq just printed an all-time high. Bonds said: not interested.

That's the signal you need to care about this morning.

Yesterday's session delivered a fractured rally - equities celebrated AI capex momentum (Micron +19.3% to $1T market cap, Nvidia pledging $150B in new spending) while the geopolitical backdrop shifted on Iran peace deal optimism. The reflex: crude crashed 3.8%. Equities rallied. Classic risk-on move.

But here's what broke the pattern:

The 10Y Treasury didn't confirm. It fell 6.5 basis points, sure, but that's not a structural pivot - that's Iran euphoria pulling a safe-haven bid out of bonds and dumping it back into risk assets. The real signal: bonds are pricing longer duration risk, not celebrating the rally.

Gold dropped 0.45% alongside the equity surge. Not a hedge move. A "safe-haven premium is deflating because the market thinks geopolitical peace breaks out" move. That's fine, except it depends entirely on Doha talks holding - and Piper Sandler is warning the Strait of Hormuz could stay closed for months anyway.

The Russell 2000 outperformed by 1.2 percentage points. That's the canary in the coal mine: small-caps leading when the Fed has 2027 hikes priced in, when energy supply is still unresolved, and when a new Fed chair (Kevin Warsh) just took the helm facing an immediate inflation shock. Small-cap outperformance usually signals confidence in the monetary backdrop. But bonds aren't confident. Bonds are saying: this Fed is going to fight inflation, not cut.

The corporate proof point: Micron's jump to $1T market cap is structural AI capex, not one-day euphoria. Nvidia's $150B spending pledge confirms it. But structural growth capex + higher-for-longer rates = duration headwinds. That's what bonds are pricing.

The second-order story nobody is covering yet: a new Fed chair arriving just as energy markets are convulsing, right when small-caps are rotating back into the mix, right when AI capex is pulling forward. That's the setup for a "growth with inflation" regime - not deflationary growth, not soft-landing cuts. That's 2027 hikes, not 2027 cuts. Bonds smelled it first.

Which market got it right - equities celebrating or bonds selling?

Watch crude at the $90 level today. If Hormuz reopening holds, energy stays crushed and bonds stay under pressure (risk stays on, inflation stays managed). If Doha breaks down, WTI snaps back above $93, energy rips, and bonds are early but right. That's the fulcrum.
