Oil Crashed 5.1%. Russell 2000 Down 1%. Futures Green. By What Algebra.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

The 10-year yield closed at 4.667% yesterday. That's a 16-month high. Equities sold off. WTI crude cratered 5.1% to $102.30. And futures are green this morning. One of these things is not like the other.

S&P 500 closed at 7,353 (-0.67%). Dow down 0.65%. Nasdaq off 0.84%. The Russell 2000 bled 1.01%, because small-caps are the canaries of domestic credit and the canaries are looking pale. The 10-year at 4.667% is the highest since before the 2024 rate-cut cycle. Every equity valuation model got its discount rate repriced overnight. The USD barely moved at 99.4, because why would it have an opinion when everything else was having one. Gold slipped 0.34% to $4,491 and held.

Bond market says higher-for-longer. Oil says the Strait of Hormuz premium is deflating. Those two reads are not compatible. The equity market, up pre-market this morning, has decided not to pick a side. That's either optimism or denial.

Here's my read:

Two forces hit markets simultaneously, and they're pointing in opposite directions. The bond market repricing for a higher-for-longer reality is the dominant signal: yields broke out to levels not seen since the pre-2024 easing cycle. That's functioning like a rising hurdle rate for every equity valuation model, especially in rate-sensitive small caps. Think of it as the discount rate on every future dollar of earnings ticking up again, compressing multiples in real time.

But oil's 5% drop tells a completely different story: the Strait of Hormuz closure risk receded, pulling energy's inflation premium out of crude. That's a split signal. Bonds say inflation persists. Oil says the acute geopolitical fear is fading. One of them is lying.

Futures this morning are betting both are wrong. The gap-up is fueled by trade deal progress and a confirmed 200-plane Boeing order from China. But APAC was red overnight (Nikkei -1.23%, ASX -1.26%), so US futures are optimism without Asian confirmation. Nvidia earnings tonight will tell you who had it right: the bond market or the oil market.

The One Trade: Long $ITA

China's confirmed 200-plane Boeing order lands on the same day defense drone demand accelerates via the Pentagon's Shield AI contract. That's a dual catalyst for aerospace and defense that the broader market selloff yesterday completely missed. Boeing and its supply chain now have real revenue catalysts, not rumors.

Confirms: ITA holds above yesterday's close in the first 30 minutes of trading and Boeing (BA) trades up more than 1.5% by 10am ET.

Kill switch: Boeing stock reverses intraday on any fresh China trade friction headline or if the plane order gets walked back to a letter of intent rather than a firm commitment.

What else I'm watching

  • $TLT is a no. The 10-year at 4.667% with upside momentum is not a dip. It's a new regime. Every basis point higher is a body blow to duration. If yields break 4.70%, inverse duration via $TBF becomes a high-conviction short through the week.
  • Stay away from small caps via $IWM. Small caps carry more floating-rate debt, so higher rates bite them faster. The Russell's 1.01% decline yesterday confirmed it. Don't buy this dip until yields stabilize or peak.
  • Oil's 5% drop is a gift for consumer discretionary and airlines. Lower crude means lower input costs across the consumer economy. $XLY becomes a relative outperformer if WTI holds below $105 through the session.
  • Position for Nasdaq upside via $QQQ, but with a tight leash. Futures up 0.69% is pricing in a decent Nvidia result. If the beat is massive, Nasdaq carries tomorrow. If it's in-line, the sell-the-news risk is real given valuation. Watch the guidance closely.
  • Watch 4.70% on the 10-year as the secondary tripwire. A clean break above that prints fresh pain for equities. Below that, the repricing narrative holds but the selloff ends here.
