The ceasefire rally this week was real. It was also a distraction.

WTI crude fell 8.73%. The S&P 500 surged 1.8% on Thursday. Markets celebrated the Iran resolution and spent the rest of the week ignoring the thing that actually matters: the June 16-17 FOMC meeting is live for a surprise hike under a brand-new Fed Chair, and May CPI just printed at 4.2% - the hottest reading in three years.

---

**Bonds weren't convinced.**

The 10-year Treasury ended the week at 4.49%, down just 5 basis points. A yield move that says "relief," not "confidence." If markets truly believed the ceasefire resolved the macro picture, yields would have dropped harder. They didn't.

**Gold sold off 2.53% to $4,215 despite a 4.2% CPI print.**

That is a market choosing the ceasefire narrative over the inflation narrative. When the classic inflation hedge sells off on the hottest print in three years, something is being mispriced. The inflation data doesn't have a 60-day expiry. The ceasefire does.

**Crude cratered 8.73% as the Hormuz war premium evaporated.**

The question is whether WTI at $84.88 reflects durable peace or a ceasefire memorandum of understanding that hasn't been formally signed yet. Those are very different things.

---

**The corporate proof point:**

SMCI fell 17% this week on a $7 billion equity issuance - the same week Alphabet and Meta announced large capital raises to fund data center buildouts. The AI trade is being diluted, not killed. The Russell 2000 gained 2.84% as megacap tech bled. The parts of the market with no AI capital raise overhang outperformed the parts that do. That rotation is worth watching.

---

**The second-order story nobody is covering:**

MSCI Emerging Markets posted the strongest regional return this week at +2.74%. Lower oil directly eases import bills across Southeast and South Asia. A softer dollar adds the currency tailwind on top. The EM relief trade is real - but it is entirely contingent on the Hormuz memorandum of understanding holding. A ceasefire breakdown reprices oil back toward $90 and hits EM from both directions simultaneously.

---

**The verdict:**

The market voted for the ceasefire over the inflation data this week. Gold agreed. Bonds were skeptical. Kevin Warsh settles the debate on June 17. Which market got it right?
