## The Brief

Three converging shocks hit markets yesterday: Alphabet and Tesla torched post-earnings on [AI](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AI) capex blowout concerns, Brent crude punched through $100 on Red Sea tanker attacks, and the White House dropped sweeping new tariffs on 60 trade partners. The cross-asset read is unambiguous: gold holding near $4,062 while the dollar barely moved and 10-year yields jumped 4.6 basis points tells you the bond market is pricing in stagflation, not a soft landing. Futures are bouncing modestly pre-open, but with KOSPI down 5.7% and Nikkei off 2.7%, the overnight signal is a relief rally built on thin air.

The S&P 500 closed at **7,408.30, down 1.21%**, with the Nasdaq leading the damage at **-2.15% to 25,137**. The Dow shed 0.97%. Small caps underperformed again, the Russell 2000 off 0.67% to 2,940. The 10-year Treasury yield climbed 4.6 basis points to **4.703%**, gold added 0.38% to close at **$4,062**, and WTI crude dropped 3.46% to $89, despite intraday Brent spiking to $102. The dollar index was essentially flat at 101.36.

Alphabet and Tesla reported Q2 results that confirmed the market's worst fear: [AI](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/AI) infrastructure spending is accelerating while near-term [ROI](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/ROI) remains opaque. Think of it like a commercial real estate developer pouring concrete on a skyscraper with no signed leases. Alphabet's capex guidance reset expectations across every cloud hyperscaler. Simultaneously, the White House enacted **10%-12.5% tariffs across 60 trade partners**, adding a fresh input-cost layer on top of energy inflation already running hot. The **Red Sea tanker strikes**, now in their 12th consecutive night, pushed Brent briefly above $100, which is the threshold where headline CPI gets re-priced across importing economies. The Fed does not cut into $100 oil and rising import tariffs. The 10-year knows this, which is why **bonds never bought the equity rally** of the prior weeks.

## What it means for you

The playbook here separates into two buckets. Defensives and real assets are the only logical shelter: gold ([GLD](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/GLD)) held its bid even as risk assets cracked, signaling genuine safe-haven demand rather than a tactical rotation. Energy ([XLE](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/XLE)) is a split story: upstream producers benefit from **$89-plus WTI** with potential for more upside if the **Bab-el-Mandeb Strait** situation worsens, but yesterday's WTI pullback from intraday highs shows the market is also pricing in demand destruction from tariff-driven slowdowns. Avoid mega-cap tech ([QQQ](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/QQQ)) until Microsoft and Amazon report next week and clarify whether capex discipline is possible. Rate-sensitive sectors like utilities ([XLU](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/XLU)) and REITs ([XLRE](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/XLRE)) face headwind from the yield jump. Defense ([ITA](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/ITA)) gets a structural tailwind from both the Middle East conflict and the NATO Brussels meeting Monday.

**S&P futures are up 0.27% pre-open to 7,465**, a modest bounce off yesterday's lows. KOSPI's **5.7% overnight collapse** is the sharpest single-market signal in the data, reflecting South Korea's acute exposure to both tariff disruption and tech export contraction. Europe is flat in early session, providing no directional conviction. The key swing factor today is the **10:00 AM New Home Sales print**. A weak number would compound the stagflation narrative by confirming that **4.70% yields are already biting** into rate-sensitive household demand. Watch whether the S&P can hold **above 7,400** on any early bounce fade.
