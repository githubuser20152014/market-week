Everyone is celebrating the Iran peace deal. Here's why you should read the fine print before joining the party.

Oil crashed 5.14% to $80.52 as Hormuz reopened. Equities ripped. The S&P 500 gaps open +2.16% pre-market to 7,595. The Nikkei surged 4.99% overnight. The KOSPI exploded 5.20%. Markets are calling this a regime change, not a one-day event. The narrative is clean, the numbers are big, and everyone who missed Friday's close is now chasing a gap open.

Three markets are not buying it — or at least, not entirely.

**Bonds** barely moved. The 10-year yield edged up just 2.4 basis points to 4.487% as equities celebrated. That is not a "growth acceleration" signal. That is a "disinflationary gift" signal. When oil drops 5% and yields barely move, the story is inflation fear gone — not economy accelerating. The distinction matters for how long this rally holds.

**Gold** added 3.41% to $4,359 on the same day oil crashed and equities ripped. Gold does not behave this way in genuine risk-on environments. It does behave this way when traders read the fine print and notice that Iran's nuclear negotiation window is 60 days unresolved and Israel has not withdrawn from seized territories. The ceasefire solved the Hormuz problem. It has not solved the other problems.

**Crude** is the obvious short, which is exactly why it's dangerous. Hormuz reopens over 30 days, not overnight. Any delay headline and crude bounces $2. The opportunity is real; pressing it is not.

The story nobody is contextualizing: SpaceX debuted at a $2.1 trillion valuation on Friday, making Musk the world's first trillionaire. Ron Baron bought $1 billion of shares in the IPO. That is a liquidity event with ripple effects into robotics, satellite infrastructure, and adjacent tech — on a day when Iran was supposed to be the only story.

The risk nobody is talking about on a peace-deal Monday: The BOJ is expected to hike to 1% tomorrow, a 31-year high. USD/JPY is sitting at 160.17. A hawkish BOJ surprise could snap the yen stronger and gut U.S. multinationals in yen-sensitive tech and autos. Everyone is watching oil. Very few are watching the yen.

So which market got it right? Equities are saying regime change. Gold is saying "we'll see." Bonds are saying disinflationary, not celebratory. One of them is wrong. The 9:15 AM ET Industrial Production print and tomorrow's FOMC will start settling it.
