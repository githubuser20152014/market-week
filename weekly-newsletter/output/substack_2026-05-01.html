Markets Rallied +2% on GDP. Gold Sold Off Into 3.5% Inflation and Day 63 of Hormuz.

The Morning Brief · 2026-05-01

The market threw a party yesterday for a GDP print. Q1 came in at +2.0%, up from a prior +0.5%, and that was enough to send all four major indices higher, Russell 2000 leading at +2.2%. Apple guided above estimates. Traders bought the growth story and called it a day. What they didn't fully price: PCE is still running at 3.5% YoY, the Strait of Hormuz is on day 63 of a blockade, and the White House is being briefed on new Iran strike options.

Gold sold off 0.7% to $4,581 into all of that. At the same time, the yen surged 2.25% to 156.58 - a textbook safe-haven move. And the 10-year barely flinched despite the GDP beat, slipping only 2.8 bps to 4.39%. Equities said risk-on. Gold, the yen, and bonds said something else entirely.

Here's my read: Institutional players used the GDP-driven rally to book profits on the geopolitical bid in gold. That is not a trend reversal - it is a reloading opportunity. The macro case hasn't changed: the Hormuz Premium is embedded in 3.5% PCE, WTI is holding at $105, and the War Powers 60-day window just expired with the Trump administration claiming a ceasefire "pause" exemption that Congress could challenge at any moment. Futures are essentially flat this morning. The market is waiting. The next Hormuz headline moves everything.
