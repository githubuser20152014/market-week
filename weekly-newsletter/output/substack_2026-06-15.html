Iran Made Peace. Musk Made Trillions. Gold Hit $4,359 Anyway.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

The peace deal worked. Oil dropped 5.14% to $80.52. The Strait of Hormuz is reopening. Inflation expectations are falling. The Fed gets to stay neutral. Equities are gapping up 2-3% pre-market. Everything is going exactly according to plan.

Gold is up 3.41% to $4,359. So either someone forgot to tell gold that the war ended, or someone read the fine print on the ceasefire and quietly decided not to celebrate.

Also: SpaceX debuted at a $2.1 trillion valuation, Elon Musk became the world's first trillionaire, and a humanoid robot ran a half-marathon faster than any human ever has. All on the same Friday. Normal week.

Here's my read:

The Hormuz Breakthrough MOU ends three months of U.S.-Iran conflict and reopens 20% of global oil supply. When that restriction lifts, energy prices collapse, inflation expectations follow, and the Fed's case for hiking evaporates. Traders are pricing a neutral FOMC at tomorrow's meeting, treating the 5.14% oil crash as the central bank's unpaid assistant. The Dow added 0.70% to 51,202, the S&P 500 gained 0.50% to 7,431, the Nasdaq lagged at +0.31% to 25,889. The 10-year yield edged up just 2.4 bps to 4.487%, which is the bond market's polite way of saying "disinflationary gift, not recession." The USD Index slipped to 99.52. All very clean. Very orderly. Very suspicious.

Gold at $4,359 with futures barely moving overnight is the tell. The smart money did not sell the peace deal. It bought more. Because Iran's nuclear negotiation window is 60 days unresolved, Israel has not withdrawn from seized territories, and oil analysts quoted overnight are already warning the "shock is far from over." The ceasefire solved the Hormuz problem. It has not solved the other problems.
