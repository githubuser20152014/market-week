Nvidia's blowout earnings and $80B buyback lit up semis and small caps yesterday. S&P up 1.08%, Russell 2000 up 2.56%, Dow crosses 50,000 for the first time. Victory lap, right?

Here's why you shouldn't trust it.

**What bonds said:** The 10-year dropped 9.5 bps to 4.57% in one day. But that's not relief. That's oscillation. Yields are still above 4.5% and the structural repricing from last week's bond rout — which touched 4.69% — is unbroken. TLT holders are not celebrating. Duration is still structurally risky.

**What gold said:** Gold finished flat at $4,534 during a 1% equity rally and a 9.5 bps yield drop. In a normal market, gold should have sold off. Instead, buyers are defending the floor. The Iran-Hormuz reprieve is temporary. The Taiwan arms package friction is real. Someone in the gold pit is pricing in a breakdown, not a peace deal.

**What crude said:** WTI held near $98, barely moving despite Iran allowing 26 tankers through the Strait of Hormuz. This is not a signal that oil risk premium is dead. This is a signal that the market is waiting for the next headline. One Trump tweet on Iran nuclear talks, and that $98 level either sinks to $90 or rallies through $100.

**The proof point:** Nvidia posted $81.6B in Q1 revenue and a 2,400% dividend hike. That's not earnings. That's a central bank liquidity injection straight into semiconductor complexity. The market is treating it as such. Semis are up. Small caps are up. The broadening is real.

But the dissenting votes are louder than the celebration. TLT says rates stay higher for longer. Gold says geopolitical risk is mispriced lower. Crude says the Iran deal is binary and unresolved.

**Which market got it right?**

The answer lands this week. Trump said he's willing to wait "a few days" on an Iran peace proposal. A deal announced before close collapses the oil risk premium, deflates gold, and validates the equity rally. A breakdown sends crude through $100, brings gold roaring back, and invalidates yesterday's Nvidia-driven broadening.

The bond market is hedging. The gold market is hedging. The crude market is hedging. Only the equity market is celebrating.
