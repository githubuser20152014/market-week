SK Hynix Raised $26.5 Billion. The Ceasefire Has Hours Left. Only One Made Headlines.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

Wall Street had a choice yesterday between watching a 48-hour ceasefire clock in the Strait of Hormuz and watching a chipmaker go public. It picked the chipmaker.

SK Hynix debuted on the Nasdaq at $149 a share, raised $26.5 billion, and got seven times oversubscribed in the process. Micron, AMD, and Marvell rode the same wave, dragging the Nasdaq up 1.3% while the S&P added 0.81% and the Russell added 1.22%. Institutional money just told you, loudly, that AI hardware demand is structural, not a fad.

Gold slipped half a percent. Yields eased 3 basis points. That is not what you'd expect on a day the market decided to celebrate, and it's the first clue the party has an expiration date. The diplomatic window that made all of this possible closes today.

Here's my read:

The setup is simple and nobody's saying it out loud: oil pulled back from a spike toward $79 because U.S. Central Command paused strikes to give diplomats a 48-hour window in the Strait of Hormuz, and that pause is the entire reason tech got to have its moment. With crude calming down, the hawkish Fed minutes from July 8, the ones flagging a possible September hike off May's 4.2% inflation print, stopped being the scariest thing in the room. Take away the ceasefire and you take away the rally's oxygen. The market priced in a peace deal that hasn't actually been signed.

This morning's futures are already getting nervous about it: Nasdaq futures down 0.56%, S&P futures barely positive. Yesterday's euphoria is getting a quiet, sober look in the mirror before the opening bell.

The One Trade: Long $XLE

Oil at $72 is pricing in a ceasefire that does not technically exist yet. The 48-hour window opened July 9 and expires today. Any breakdown in talks sends WTI back toward $79 and energy equities gap higher on the news, not after it.

Confirms: WTI crude holds above $71.50 through the 10:00 AM ET New Home Sales print, and XLE opens above its prior session close with volume tracking above 30-day average by 11:00 AM ET.

Kill switch: A confirmed ceasefire agreement or joint diplomatic statement from U.S. and Iranian negotiators before noon ET collapses the geopolitical premium in oil, and you want to be out fast.

What else I'm watching

  • SOXX / SMH: Hold what you have, but don't you dare chase the open. The semiconductor rally was real, but futures are already fading it 0.56% pre-market. Wait for a dip toward yesterday's lows before adding.
  • XLE: This is the trade nobody is talking about because everyone's watching chips. Asymmetric setup: downside is a ceasefire clipping a few percent, upside is escalation repricing the whole sector.
  • TLT: Stay away from it. A 3 basis point dip on a relief trade is not a regime change, and the Fed is still talking about hiking in September.
  • GLD: Watch $4,100 like a hawk. If equities open soft today and gold still can't bounce back toward $4,120, the safe-haven bid is genuinely cracking.
  • ITA / XAR: NATO just pledged 70 billion euros to Ukraine with a 5% GDP defense spending target attached. This is a multi-year tailwind, not a headline trade. Build on weakness.
