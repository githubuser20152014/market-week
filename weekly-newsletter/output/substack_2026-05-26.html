America Bombed Iran. Oil Fell 3.4%. The Market Read It As De-Escalation.

The Morning Brief - Market intelligence at the open

America bombed Iran's missile sites near the Strait of Hormuz yesterday. Oil fell 3.4%. Markets called it "de-escalation." An LNG tanker transited the Strait for the first time since February, and traders priced out the supply premium before Iran finished writing its retaliation vow. Doha peace talks are running. CENTCOM strikes are running. Both. Simultaneously.

Markets picked the optimistic read and ran with it, because that's what markets do until they don't.

Here's my read:

US equities closed green across the board, led by Russell 2000 up +0.91% to 2,869 - small caps winning while the Strait of Hormuz is a live military zone, which tells you something about what's priced in. Dow +0.58%. S&P +0.37% to 7,473. The real move: WTI crude collapsed 3.36% to $93.35 on that tanker signal. Gold refused to budge at $4,524 (buyers defending the floor even during active US airstrikes), the 10Y yield slipped to 4.558%, and the dollar index dropped to 99.07. Risk-on. Apparently.

The catalyst: Iran vowed retaliation overnight. Petraeus called their posture "blinking." S&P futures are now +0.54% at 7,531. Someone is wrong about what "blinking" means, and we find out which at market open.

The One Trade: Long $GLD

Gold held $4,524 through live US military strikes and a risk-on equity rally - buyers are refusing to let it sell off. Iran's overnight retaliation vow is the asymmetric catalyst that reprices it higher today.

Confirms: GLD holds above yesterday's close in the first 30 minutes of trading and moves up while equities open flat or soft on any Iran headline.

Kill switch: Iran stands down publicly before noon, Doha talks produce a signed framework, and oil reverses higher - that risk-off unwind sends GLD below $4,480 fast.

What else I'm watching

  • Fade $XLE on the open: Hormuz supply premium is deflating. WTI at $93 with tankers moving is a structural headwind. Energy names are vulnerable and Iran's retaliation adds two-sided headline risk.
  • $IWM for continuation: Small caps led yesterday at +0.91%. Domestic growth rotation is valid as energy costs fall. Hold through 10 AM if the gap-up holds and no Iran escalation drops.
  • Avoid duration rallies: Fed Chair Warsh has January 2027 hike baked in. $TLT is not your friend. This is a ceiling on bond yields, not a floor.
  • Monitor $USO intraday: WTI already down 3.4%. If Iran retaliates or Hormuz transits break again, oil snaps back violently. A close above $96 flips the energy trade back on.
  • Watch 9:15 AM Industrial Production: First domestic data print. A miss undercuts the futures enthusiasm in seconds.
