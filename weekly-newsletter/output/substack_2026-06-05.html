54% of an Index. 2 Stocks. 1 Broadcom Miss. Math Class Was in Session.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

Broadcom told the world it expected $16 billion in AI-related revenue next quarter. The world expected more. That gap - not a earnings miss, not a guidance collapse, just a slight shortfall in one company's AI forecast - wiped $285 billion in market cap overnight and sent shockwaves straight to Seoul.

The KOSPI fell 5.5% to 8,160 before 9:15 AM local time. The Korea Exchange activated a sell-side sidecar - suspended program trading entirely - as Samsung shed 6.7% and SK Hynix plunged 8.8%. Jensen Huang, CEO of Nvidia, had flown into Seoul that same morning and told reporters he had "some surprises" prepared. The KOSPI did not wait to find out what they were.

This is what a 54% concentration looks like when the thesis cracks. Two stocks. One trade. One Broadcom miss. Math class was in session, and Korea did not study.

Here's my read:

Yesterday's US close was a split decision. The Dow added +1.73% to 51,562. Russell 2000 gained +1.45% to 2,935. Rate-sensitive cyclicals pricing in a soft jobs number. The Nasdaq closed -0.09% at 26,831 - flat enough that it barely registered as a move. The S&P inched +0.41% to 7,584. That is three indices pointing one direction and the tech index pointing nowhere. This morning it is worse: S&P futures -0.37%, Nasdaq futures -0.87%, Dow futures barely positive at +0.15%. The tape is not unified. It is waiting.

What it is waiting for is the 8:30 AM ET May NFP print. Consensus expects 85K to 115K jobs, unemployment steady at 4.3%. The S&P's entire push toward 7,600 has been priced on one assumption: the economy cools fast enough to force Fed cuts but not fast enough to break anything. The 10-year yield eased to 4.477% yesterday. Gold held at $4,494. WTI slipped to $92.86. The dollar softened to 99.18. That cross-asset picture is a perfectly assembled rate-cut argument. The NFP number will confirm it or destroy it.

The gold signal is the one worth flagging. Gold held $4,494 on a Dow up day. That is not a risk-on rally dragging precious metals along for the ride. That is a separate, independent bid. Buyers are treating $4,490 as a structural floor, not a ceiling - with the Hormuz premium still live, the dollar soft, and 20 consecutive sessions of foreign selling hammering Seoul into the pavement. When safe havens hold while risk assets party, one of them is wrong about the future.

The One Trade: Long $TLT

The 10-year yield sits at 4.477% the morning NFP is expected to print soft. A sub-85K number sends duration on a direct flight to 4.30%. TLT captures the full repricing with zero equity correlation risk on a day when the Nasdaq is already limping into the open.

Confirms: TLT trades above $96.50 within 30 minutes of the 8:30 AM print and the 10-year yield drops below 4.40%.

Kill switch: NFP prints above 115K and the 10-year yield spikes above 4.55%. The rate-cut narrative evaporates. TLT falls through $94 fast. Get out fast.
