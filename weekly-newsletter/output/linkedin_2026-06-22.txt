The equity market agrees with the oil traders. The rest of the tape is not so sure.

---

The dissenting markets are each saying something specific this morning.

Bonds: T-Note futures dropped 0.5% pre-market, even as equities celebrated. The bond market is not pricing a peace deal. It is pricing Kevin Warsh's hawkish hold at 3.75% and a dot-plot that whispers about a 2026 rate hike. When bonds and equities disagree on risk, one of them is right and one of them gets repriced.

Gold: $4,227. Flat. In a broad risk-on session with a slightly firmer dollar and equity futures screaming higher. Gold did not sell. When institutional money keeps the insurance policy active during a rally, it is telling you something about conviction levels. The smart money is not fully buying the Burgenstock narrative yet.

Crude: WTI fell 1.88% to $75.16 on the day Iran closed the Strait of Hormuz. The crude market is not pricing a supply disruption. It is pricing a diplomatic resolution from Swiss-mediated US-Iran talks that has not been officially announced yet. That is a bold bet. If it is wrong, energy reprices violently to the upside and equities follow it lower.

---

The budget airline story this weekend is the macro thesis in microcosm. Ultra-low-cost carriers are running out of runway as consumers trade down in fares even as operating costs stay elevated. This is not an airline problem. It is a consumer stress problem: the cohort that drives domestic travel and discretionary spending is getting squeezed, and the rate environment is not giving them a lifeline. This is exactly the kind of second-order signal that the headline indices are ignoring this morning.

---

The angle nobody is covering: the AI power wall. The Department of Energy has flagged that data center electricity demands are outstripping regional grid capacity. The software-only AI trade has hit a physical bottleneck. If Anthropic's models come back online under a federal monitoring framework as part of the Trump de-escalation, the power draw requirements become even more acute. The investment opportunity has quietly shifted from model builders to grid builders: utility infrastructure, nuclear co-location, and clean energy integration are becoming the structural trade of the AI cycle.
