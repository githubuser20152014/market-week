The Ceasefire Had a Great 24 Hours. Gold Hit $4,727 Anyway.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

Yesterday the equity market decided the Hormuz ceasefire was probably fine and started pricing in the good ending. Then U.S. and Iranian forces exchanged actual fire - both sides claiming the other shot first - and WTI crude, which had briefly dipped on peace-deal optimism, remembered that the Strait handles 20% of global oil. S&P 500 down 0.38% to 7,337. Russell 2000 down 1.63% - worst of the majors - because small-caps are the canaries of domestic credit and consumer health, and right now they are canaries with a cough.

Meanwhile: gold printed a new record at $4,727. Dollar down 0.33% to 97.93. Ten-year yield up 3.6 bps to 4.392%. The equity market wanted to believe in miracles. The bond market, the commodity complex, and the currency market all voted no. When the "safe" asset hits all-time highs on a day equities are only down 0.4%, that is not coincidence. That is capital making a decision and not being subtle about it.

Then Trump gave the EU a July 4th deadline - sign a trade deal or face "much higher" tariffs. Cloudflare fell 18% after cutting 1,100 jobs because AI "fundamentally changes" how it works. CoreWeave fell 10% on weak revenue guidance. Three coffin nails, one session. And April Nonfarm Payrolls land at 8:30 AM ET this morning, consensus 65k, with the power to erase the pre-market bounce before most people finish their first cup of coffee.

Here's my read:

The Hormuz ceasefire is a fiction held together by press releases and mutual exhaustion. U.S. and Iranian forces trading fire - each insisting the other pulled the trigger first - is not how ceasefires hold. It is how they end. WTI at $94.37 is the market's honest assessment. The brief dip that followed last session's peace-deal optimism has been entirely retraced. The NACHO trade - "Not A Chance Hormuz Opens" - is the bet Wall Street is now pricing as structural, not temporary. Shipping, fertilizer, energy ETFs: the war premium is not going away quietly.

The earnings story is equally ugly and gets less attention than it deserves. Cloudflare and CoreWeave both missed revenue guidance on the same evening, and both companies cited AI efficiency as the reason for their layoffs. Read that twice: they are cutting headcount because of AI while missing the revenue targets that were supposed to justify the AI buildout. That is not a growth story. That is cannibalism. The AI infrastructure consensus - the one that has kept SKYY and CLOU elevated - just took its first real structural hits. It may survive. It is not uninjured.

The macro setup going into today's open is genuinely binary on NFP at 8:30 AM ET. Consensus is 65k after ADP came in soft at 108k. Sub-50k triggers stagflation alarms and likely reverses the pre-market bounce. Above 100k gives bulls a real catalyst and could temporarily pressure gold. Watch 7,350 on the S&P as the line between a genuine gap-and-hold and a failed recovery that retests yesterday's lows before lunch.

Gold is printing record highs while the dollar falls, equities slip, and a live military exchange in the Strait of Hormuz keeps the geopolitical floor firmly under the bid. This is not speculative momentum - it is capital rotating into the one asset that benefits from every bad outcome on the table today.
