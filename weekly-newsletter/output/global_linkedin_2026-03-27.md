# LinkedIn Post — Global Investor Edition | March 28, 2026

---

This week, oil nearly hit $100 a barrel. Stocks fell 2%. Bond yields rose. All at once.

That combination has a name: **stagflation risk** — where prices keep climbing even as growth slows. It's the scenario central banks dread most. This week it moved from background worry to front-page concern.

Here's what stood out:

🛢️ **WTI Crude +7.5% to $99.64** — fears of supply disruption through the Strait of Hormuz (roughly 20% of the world's seaborne oil passes through it daily) triggered a sharp spike. Oil is sometimes called a "tax on everything" — when crude rises, inflation pressures follow.

📉 **S&P 500 -1.9% | Nasdaq & Dow -2.1%** — broad selloff, no sector was spared. VIX jumped 10% to 27.4, meaning institutions were actively buying portfolio insurance.

📈 **10Y Treasury yield +8bps to 4.42% | 30Y +13bps to 4.78%** — the bond market is pricing out rate cuts. Higher yields compress equity valuations — particularly painful for growth stocks.

🌏 **Asia held up — and then some** — Hang Seng +3.5%, Nikkei +2.0%, ASX +0.8%. Money is rotating out of expensive US equities into cheaper Asian alternatives. A global investor who held EWH or EWJ this week was cushioned from the US selloff.

🥇 **Gold -0.8%** — counterintuitive given the risk-off environment, but explainable: when bond yields spike, traders sell profitable gold positions to cover losses elsewhere. Forced liquidation, not a macro reversal. The fundamentals for gold remain intact.

---

**The takeaway for long-term investors:**

The era of simply holding US large-cap growth and watching it compound is facing a real test. That doesn't mean abandon the plan — it means check your concentration.

Three positioning moves worth considering:
→ Trim overweight US growth (SPY, QQQ) — valuations offer less cushion with yields at 4.42% and oil near $100
→ Add Asia-Pacific exposure (EWH for Hong Kong, EWJ for Japan) — the relative momentum is clear
→ Stay short on bond duration — the 30-year at 4.78% on a steepening curve is not the place to reach for yield right now

The long-term investor's edge is using volatility as a signal to rebalance deliberately — not a reason to panic.
