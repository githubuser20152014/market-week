Iran Rejected the Peace Deal. Trump Rejected Iran. Gold Dropped 0.89% Anyway.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

The Dow added 0.02% on Friday. Two basis points. Not a conviction, a shrug dressed up as a close. Meanwhile, Nasdaq ripped 1.71% to 26,247, KOSPI went up 4.32% while New York slept, oil jumped 2.33%, and Spirit Airlines quietly stopped existing.

Iran rejected the peace deal. Trump rejected Iran. Netanyahu said the war is "not over." And gold, which you would expect to be screaming higher in this environment, dropped 0.89% to $4,678 for its second consecutive day of selling. The market is not panicking. The market is rotating. Into the disruption itself.

That rotation - from macro-panic gold into direct energy exposure - is the entire story of this session. The Strait of Hormuz is a toll booth that just closed, and the smart money is buying the toll booth operator, not the safe haven.

Here's my read:

The IEA called the Strait of Hormuz closure "the biggest supply shock in history." Saudi Aramco's response: a 26% Q1 profit surge, because their pipeline runs around the Strait. Every tanker rerouted away from the Strait is direct revenue to the pipeline-adjacent plays. That is not a temporary price spike. That is a structural revenue event that lasts as long as the Strait stays closed, and neither Trump nor Netanyahu gave any indication it closes soon.

The cross-asset read confirms the thesis. WTI crude +2.33% to $97.64 while 10Y Treasury yield fell only 2.8 bps to 4.364% - bonds barely caught a bid on a day with active war escalation. That is the tell: energy-driven inflation is keeping the Fed sidelined, and the bond market knows it. Gold at $4,678 is not breaking down; it is the market saying "I don't need the panic hedge when I can own the supply shock directly." USD Index edged up a fractional 0.13% to 97.96. Pre-market is flat: S&P futures 7,415 (-0.05%), Nasdaq 29,303 (-0.10%).

The overnight anomaly: KOSPI surged 4.32% on Friday, the loudest signal in the session. Find the catalyst before the open - that kind of move in a major index does not happen without a reason. Europe opened dead flat, consistent with wait-and-see mode after the Iran collapse. The swing factor today is simple: any signal of resumed US-Iran negotiations pulls oil lower and lifts beaten-down transports. Any hardening of Netanyahu's posture pushes WTI back toward $100. Watch $98.50 on WTI as the line between "contained" and "back to the prior highs."

The One Trade: Long $XLE

Aramco's 26% Q1 profit surge on pipeline rerouting confirms the Strait of Hormuz closure is a revenue event for integrated energy, not just a price spike. The fundamental bid is real. Iran talks just collapsed again. The market is pricing this correctly for once.

Confirms: XLE holds above Friday's close and WTI crude stays above $96.50 through the first hour of trading.
