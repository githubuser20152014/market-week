_A weekly breakdown of what just happened in global markets — and what to do about it._

Hot take: "the market fell this week" isn't the whole story. Not even close. 🙃

Here's what actually happened:

*US stocks fell 2%.* S&P 500, Nasdaq, Dow — all down. Fine. Normal. Scary. Whatever.

*Oil nearly hit $100 a barrel.* Up 7.5% in a week. The trigger? Fears over the Strait of Hormuz — that tiny waterway that 20% of the world's seaborne oil squeezes through every single day. Traders didn't wait to see if it would actually get blocked. They just… panicked. Prices spiked. You're welcome, petrol station.

*The 10-year yield climbed to 4.42%.* Which is the bond market's polite way of saying "we don't think inflation is dead, please compensate us accordingly." Rate cut dreams: fading.

Put those three things together and you get a word economists love dusting off: *stagflation*. Prices rising. Growth slowing. Central banks stuck. It's the financial equivalent of being stuck in traffic AND late for something important AND your AC is broken.

Now here's the part the headlines buried:

*Asia didn't care.* 😐

Hang Seng: +3.5%. Nikkei: +2%. ASX: +0.8%.

While Wall Street was having a moment, Hong Kong and Tokyo were just quietly going up. Global money is rotating out of expensive US tech into cheaper Asian alternatives — and this week it showed.

Oh, and gold *fell* during a geopolitical oil spike. Which feels wrong. It is slightly wrong — but it's a "forced selling" thing, not a "gold is dead" thing.
