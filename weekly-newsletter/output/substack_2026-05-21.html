Nvidia Lit It Up. Small Caps Followed. Gold Said "Thanks, I'll Stay Right Here."

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

The dominant mood is cautious relief: Nvidia's blowout earnings and $80B buyback lit up semis and small caps yesterday, but the rally carries a short shelf life. Gold pinned near $4,534, the dollar holding firm, 10-year yields still elevated at 4.57% despite a 9.5 bps pullback. This is the smell of easy money already made.

The KOSPI's extraordinary +8.4% surge overnight is the loudest cross-asset signal: Iran allowing 26 tankers through the Strait of Hormuz, Trump signaling patience on nuclear talks, US-China peace talk optimism. Yet Europe is flatlined and Nasdaq futures are slightly red this morning. Futures point to a near-flat open.

The question: which market got it right?

Here's my read:

The S&P 500 closed at 7,432.97, up 1.08%, while the Russell 2000 led the board with a +2.56% surge to 2,817 as smaller-cap names played catch-up to the AI-driven rally. The Dow crossed 50,000 for the first time, closing at 50,009. The 10-year Treasury yield dropped 9.5 basis points to 4.572%: temporary relief after last week's bond rout that briefly touched 4.69%. Gold barely moved, closing at $4,533.70, and WTI crude held near $98.32.

Two catalysts drove the session. First, Nvidia posted $81.6B in Q1 revenue and announced an $80B share buyback plus a 2,400% dividend hike, validating the AI infrastructure thesis like a central bank injecting liquidity directly into semis. Second, geopolitical temperature dropped: Iran allowed 26 tankers through the Strait of Hormuz, Trump signaled patience on nuclear talks. The combination of cooling yields, the Nvidia beat, and the Hormuz reprieve gave bulls enough cover to run. The Russell's outperformance tells you this was broad, not just mega-cap window dressing.

But here's the tension: bonds never fully bought the rally. TLT remains structurally challenged with yields above 4.5%. And gold (flat close during a 1% equity rally) is a red flag for bears. If the geopolitical reprieve is temporary, which the Taiwan arms package friction and Iran's "controlled maritime zone" language suggest it is, gold has a floor. Someone is pricing in a breakdown.

The One Trade: Long $GLD

Gold held $4,533 through a 1% equity rally and a 9.5 bps yield drop. This means buyers are defending the floor against tailwinds, not just a bid of last resort. Iran's 'controlled maritime zone' language and the Taiwan arms package friction give this trade a catalyst within days.

Confirms: GLD holds above yesterday's close through the 11am ET check while Iran headlines remain unresolved. Any headline suggesting talks are stalling is an immediate accelerant.

Kill switch: A confirmed Iran nuclear deal is announced before the close, collapsing the geopolitical risk premium and sending GLD below the $4,500 level on volume. Get out fast.

What else I'm watching

  • Semis (SOXX/SMH): Hold but don't chase. Nvidia's earnings are fully priced in at these levels. Trim into intraday strength above yesterday's close.
  • Small caps (IWM): Watch for a second-day follow-through above 2,817 on the Russell. Outperforming by 1.5 percentage points signals genuine broadening, not mega-cap squeeze. If IWM holds through noon, that's a real rotation signal worth adding to.
  • Bonds (TLT): Avoid duration adds until the 10-year prints below 4.5% on a closing basis. The single 9.5 bps bounce doesn't reverse structural repricing. Don't fight the Fed and the deficit simultaneously.
  • Energy (XLE): Two-way trade on the Iran headline. A nuclear deal confirmed = crude drops and XLE sells off sharply. Talks stall or collapse = Hormuz risk premium returns, crude through $100, XLE becomes a buy. Position size should reflect the binary.
