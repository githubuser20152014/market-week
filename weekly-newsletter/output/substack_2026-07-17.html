Netflix Missed Revenue by 1%. The Market Took 9% Off the Top.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

The June ceasefire between the U.S. and Iran is dead. The Strait of Hormuz has a live naval blockade. And yesterday the bond market told you exactly what kind of crisis this is: yields rose while equities sold off and gold refused to budge from $4,001.

That's not a growth scare. That's an oil-driven inflation re-acceleration signal, and the standard flight-to-bonds playbook just broke on camera.

S&P futures are off 0.9%, Nasdaq futures are down 1.7%, and the KOSPI collapsed 6.4% overnight. WTI at $79.86 is the number that settles today's argument. Stagflation doesn't announce itself politely.

Here's my read:

Yesterday's US close was ugly at the index level, uglier under the hood. The S&P 500 fell 0.5% to 7,533, the Nasdaq dropped 1.5% to 25,882, and the Dow shed 0.2% to 52,553. Small caps went nowhere, closing flat at 2,974. The real story was cross-asset: gold held $4,001, the 10Y yield ticked up 2.4 basis points to 4.569%, WTI crude surged 1.2% to $79.86, and the USD index barely budged at 100.78. Bonds and gold refused to sell off while equities fell, which is the classic stress signal, not the classic risk-off one.

Two catalysts did the damage. First, the June ceasefire officially collapsed on July 16 when the U.S. launched airstrikes on Iranian military installations near the Strait of Hormuz and reinstated a naval blockade of Iranian ports. Iran retaliated with drone and missile strikes on U.S. bases in Jordan, Kuwait, and Bahrain, and declared the strait a red line. Think of the Strait of Hormuz as a one-lane bridge carrying 20% of the world's seaborne oil: any credible threat to close it is a supply shock priced in immediately. Second, Netflix dropped 9% after-hours on a Q3 revenue guide of $12.86 billion against a $13.00 billion consensus, a 1% miss that dragged the entire consumer discretionary and ad-tech complex lower into this morning. The market did not grade on a curve.

The One Trade: Long $XLE

The Strait of Hormuz naval blockade is live and Iran has declared it a red line: every day this escalation holds is another day the market has to price a genuine oil supply disruption, and WTI is already at $79.86 heading into an open where futures confirm the bid.

Confirms: WTI crude breaks and holds above $80 by 10:30 AM ET, with XLE trading above its prior-day close on above-average volume.
Kill switch: WTI reverses below $78 intraday, get out fast, that's the risk-off impulse overwhelming even the supply shock premium.

What else I'm watching

  • Long $XLE or $XOP: the Hormuz escalation is a structural supply threat, not noise, and don't you dare sell it just because it already moved.
  • Stay away from $QQQ: Netflix's miss confirms growth-at-any-price is cracking, and tech was already leaning on AI capex assumptions before this print landed.
  • Hold $GLD: gold sat above $4,000 through the entire risk-off session. This is a geopolitical and inflation hedge that is working. Do not take profits ahead of a weekend with active military escalation unresolved.
  • The trade nobody is talking about: $TLT is not the safe haven it looks like. Yields rose even as equities sold off, which means bonds are pricing inflation risk, not a growth collapse.
