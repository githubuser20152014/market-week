The S&P fell 0.38% yesterday. Futures are up 0.5% this morning. Everyone is calling it a healthy pullback.

They're wrong about what it is.

---

Gold closed at a new record: $4,727. Not because of momentum. Because the commodity complex read the same ceasefire headlines as equities and came to the exact opposite conclusion. When the "fear asset" hits all-time highs on a day equities drop less than half a percent, that is capital making a statement. The smart money voted with its wallet. The wallet voted for gold.

Bonds added 3.6 basis points on the 10-year, closing at 4.392%. Yields do not rise in a clean risk-off move. The market is pricing in something more complicated: inflation persistence, energy shock risk, and a Fed that cannot cut into $94 oil without reigniting the problem it spent two years fixing. The bond market is not confused. It is telling you the Fed is stuck.

Crude sits at $94.37, and the brief dip on peace-deal optimism has been entirely retraced. U.S. and Iranian forces exchanged fire in the Strait of Hormuz - each side claiming the other pulled the trigger first. Wall Street has a name for this now: the NACHO trade. "Not A Chance Hormuz Opens." The war premium is not temporary. It is structural.

---

Then earnings delivered the subplot nobody wanted. Cloudflare cut 1,100 jobs and fell 18%. CoreWeave missed revenue guidance and fell 10%. Same evening. Both companies cited AI efficiency as the reason for the layoffs - meaning they are cutting headcount because of AI while missing the revenue targets that were supposed to justify the AI buildout. That is not a growth story. Two AI infrastructure names cracking in one session is the first real test of the consensus that AI capex is recession-proof. The consensus did not pass cleanly.

---

The story getting buried under Hormuz noise: Toyota reported Q4 profit down 49% on direct U.S. tariff impact. This is not an isolated result. Tariff drag on multinational industrial earnings is a template. Watch how Japan-exposed names trade today. That will tell you whether the market is pricing this as a one-off or the beginning of a pattern.

---

So which market got it right yesterday? Gold at a record. Crude holding $94. Bonds pricing persistent inflation. Or S&P futures up 0.5% this morning?

One of them is going to look very wrong by end of session. April Nonfarm Payrolls print at 8:30 AM ET, consensus 65k. The number does not lie.
