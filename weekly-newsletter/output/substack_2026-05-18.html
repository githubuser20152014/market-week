Someone Hit a Nuclear Plant. Gold Closed Flat. The 10-Year Didn't.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

Someone bombed a nuclear power plant yesterday. The 10-year yield jumped 13 basis points. And gold did nothing.

That is not a typo. An Iranian drone struck the UAE's Barakah Nuclear Power Plant. Trump posted "the clock is ticking, move FAST or risk total destruction" on Truth Social. The G7 finance ministers are meeting in Paris today specifically to model what a prolonged Strait of Hormuz closure does to global energy costs. Ryanair's CFO used the word "armageddon."

And gold closed flat at $4,556. When gold stops going down on catastrophic news, it means gold is done going down.

Here's my read:

This is the 1970s stagflation playbook running live in 2026, and Friday's data made it official. PPI came in at 6.0% year-over-year on May 14 - the print that quietly killed the Fed's rate-cut story - and the bond market repriced immediately: the 10-year spiked 13.4 basis points to 4.595% in a single session, the sharpest move in weeks. The S&P 500 fell 1.24% to 7,408. The Nasdaq dropped 1.54% to 26,225. Small caps got the worst of it: Russell 2000 down 2.44% to 2,793, because small-caps are the canaries of domestic credit, and right now they are canaries with a cough.

Here is the contradiction that tells you everything: WTI crude fell 3.34% to $101.90 even as Iran threatened the Strait of Hormuz. The market is saying demand destruction now outweighs supply disruption risk. That is a recession signal dressed up in geopolitical clothing. The BoE and ECB are signaling June hikes into stalling GDP. This is not a garden-variety risk-off session. This is a macro regime change.

The One Trade: Long $GLD

Gold refused to sell at $4,556 while equities dropped 1.2%, yields spiked 13 basis points, and a nuclear facility got hit by a drone. When an asset stops going down on the worst possible news, the next move is up.

Confirms: GLD holds above $4,556 in the first 30 minutes of trading and shows relative strength versus SPY. A push above $4,570 intraday confirms buyers are stepping in.

Kill switch: Gold breaks below $4,530 on a sharp dollar reversal or a surprise de-escalation statement from Tehran before 10 AM ET - either pulls the geopolitical premium out fast. Get out fast.

What else I'm watching

  • Don't you dare touch long-duration right now. $TLT and $EDV are live grenades. PPI at 6% has the Fed boxed out of cutting, and every basis point higher in yields is more duration pain. The bond market is not your friend today.
  • Energy is a conditional trade, not a conviction trade. $XLE and $XOP spike hard on a Hormuz headline, fall back to $98 WTI if the G7 signals diplomatic resolution. Wait for the signal before positioning. Blind long energy here is a coin flip.
  • The trade nobody is talking about: agriculture. The US-China $17B soybean and rare earths deal is real and largely ignored by a market fixated on Iran. $MOO and $WEAT are the cleanest beneficiary in a news cycle that otherwise has nothing good in it.
  • Defensives are the adult table. $XLP and $XLU are under-owned after the mega-cap tech run and offer yield-adjacent income profiles that compete well if the 10-year holds below 4.7%. Stay defensive.
  • Watch Industrial Production at 9:15 AM ET. A weak print confirms stagflation and cyclicals get hit. A beat buys a brief rally - and solves absolutely nothing, because the 10-year is still at 4.595%.
