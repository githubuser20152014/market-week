Yesterday looked like a clean risk-on session. Ceasefire. Google chip order. Nasdaq up 0.86%. KOSPI surging 8.18% overnight.

Here's why you shouldn't trust it.

---

BONDS VOTED NO.

The 10Y ticked up 1.6 bps to 4.552% - not a panic move, but a signal. Bonds don't rally into rate cuts when Cleveland Fed Nowcasting is calling 4.18% CPI for tomorrow. If the session were a genuine broad rotation, yields would be flat or lower. They weren't.

---

GOLD VOTED HARDER.

$4,358 on ceasefire day, with equities gapping up, is the tell of the session. Gold is supposed to sell when "risk-on" kicks in. It added $22 instead. That's real-money allocation treating gold as inflation insurance, not war speculation. The buyers didn't get the memo that the war risk went away - or they read the CPI forecast and didn't care.

---

CRUDE VOTED FOR NEITHER CAMP.

WTI gave back $2.15 on the Hormuz relief trade - that's the ceasefire doing what it was supposed to do. But $89.34 after the war premium unwinds is still $15 above pre-conflict levels. The ceasefire refunded part of the fear tax. Not all of it. The Strait remains restricted. A pause is not a deal.

---

THE CORPORATE PROOF POINT.

Corning just signed another multibillion-dollar AI infrastructure deal with Amazon - their second major pact this cycle. That is the fundamental anchor beneath yesterday's Google chip order story. The hyperscalers are not slowing AI capex; they are accelerating it. That is what moved semiconductors, that is what moved KOSPI, and that is the trade the market is chasing. The infrastructure buildout is real. The question is what happens to that narrative when CPI prints 4.18% and Warsh has to respond on June 17.

---

THE SECOND-ORDER STORY NOBODY IS COVERING.

The Pentagon quietly added Alibaba and Baidu to its China military-linked firms list - on the same day as the ceasefire announcement. The market is pricing the diplomatic win and ignoring the tech cold war escalation happening simultaneously. Nvidia's CEO declining Senate testimony on AI and China exports this week is another data point in the same direction. The ceasefire is a pause in one conflict. The tech cold war does not pause.

---

WHICH MARKET GOT IT RIGHT?

The Nasdaq celebrated the catalyst. Bonds flagged the inflation risk. Gold bought the inflation hedge. Crude gave back the fear premium but kept the structural premium.

My read: the Nasdaq won the session. Gold wins the week. CPI at 4.18% tomorrow is the deciding vote - and Warsh's June 17 debut either confirms "higher for longer" is back on the table or delivers the dovish surprise nobody is currently modeling.
