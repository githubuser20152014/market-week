The era of cheap, venture-capital-subsidized convenience is officially over. 

For a decade, we lived in an anomaly. Uber, DoorDash, and budget airlines were artificially cheap because of two things: 
1. Sub-$70 oil. 
2. Record-low interest rates that allowed tech companies to burn cash for "growth." 

Both are now gone. 

With WTI crude holding near $100/bbl due to the Iran conflict/Strait of Hormuz disruption, the "input cost" for the gig economy has exploded. The result? A "Convenience Tax" that is quietly hitting your wallet through the "plumbing" of your apps.

- Lyft/Uber fuel surcharges: +$0.55/ride.
- DoorDash/UberEats "fuel assistance" fees: +$0.45-$2.00.
- Gas pump jump: +$0.90/gallon since last year.

For the average household, this adds up to roughly **$553 per year** in extra costs just to maintain the same lifestyle.

This isn't just a temporary spike. It's a structural shift. We are returning to a world where "instant" and "delivered" are luxury services, not defaults.
