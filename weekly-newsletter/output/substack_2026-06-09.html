Google Ordered the Chips. KOSPI Bounced 8.18%. Gold Still Held $4,358.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

The good news came all at once yesterday. A Trump-brokered Israel-Iran ceasefire removed the Strait of Hormuz blockade scenario from the risk deck. WTI fell $2.15 as the fear tax got partially refunded. And a Google chip order the size of a small country's quarterly GDP gave the semiconductor crowd a reason to celebrate. The Nasdaq hit 25,929 (+0.86%). KOSPI bounced 8.18% overnight. Futures are pricing a gap-up open.

Gold added $22 and held $4,358. On ceasefire day. With equities gapping up. The metal that is supposed to sell when "risk-on" kicks in didn't sell. That is not a rounding error. That is the market telling you it doesn't believe the inflation story is over - because it isn't.

Cleveland Fed Nowcasting is calling 4.18% for tomorrow's CPI. Kevin Warsh holds his first Fed meeting June 17. The ceasefire bought one good session. The data doesn't care about diplomatic breakthroughs.

Here's my read:

Yesterday's session was a narrow tech story, not a broad risk-on rotation. The S&P added 0.30% to 7,405. The Russell 2000 added 0.77%. The Dow went negative at 50,786. The spread between those three numbers tells you exactly who the buyers were: semiconductor funds and AI momentum traders chasing a Google catalyst, not generalist allocators rotating back into equities. WTI crude at $89.34 after giving back the Hormuz war premium is still $15 above pre-conflict levels - the ceasefire refunded part of the fear tax, not all of it. The 10Y ticked up 1.6 bps to 4.552% because the bond market runs its own inflation model and it doesn't like what it sees. The dollar slipped to 99.78 on relative softness, which ordinarily would send gold lower. It didn't. Three different markets are all saying the same thing: 4.18% CPI tomorrow is the only number that matters this week.

The Pentagon adding Alibaba and Baidu to its China military-linked firms list is the trade war escalation nobody is covering right now because the ceasefire headline is louder. It shouldn't be ignored. The diplomatic thaw was already thin, and this puts Chinese tech ADRs on notice. The market is pricing it at zero today. That's usually when it bites.

The One Trade: Long $GLD

Gold is holding $4,358 through a ceasefire and a risk-on futures gap, confirming buyers are defending the floor as an inflation hedge, not a war bet, with CPI at 4.18% sitting 24 hours out.

Confirms: $GLD holds above the $4,340 level through the first hour of trading (10:30 AM ET) as equity futures remain green. Gold refusing to give back even 0.5% on ceasefire news is the confirmation the bid is real.

Kill switch: Trade is invalidated if CPI expectations shift sharply dovish intraday - a Fed speaker walking back the hawkish line - and GLD breaks below $4,310. That would signal the inflation hedge thesis is unwinding. Get out fast.

The structural case: 46 of 68 global central banks are still overshooting their inflation targets. The Fed's new chair has not yet proven he will hold the line. A 4.18% CPI print tomorrow is not the kind of number that sends gold lower. The smart money didn't sell the peace. It bought the inflation.
