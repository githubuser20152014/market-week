4.2% CPI Killed the Rate Cut. Then Iran Closed 20% of Global Oil Supply. Futures: +1%.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

May CPI printed 4.2% year-over-year. A three-year high. Then the U.S. struck Iranian targets near Bandar Abbas, and Tehran declared the Strait of Hormuz closed. The S&P dropped 1.6%. Gold barely blinked.

This morning, futures are up 1%.

Someone in this room is wrong.

Here's my read:

Yesterday was a two-punch knockout. S&P 500: 7,266.99, down 1.6%. Nasdaq: down 2.0% to 25,169. The Dow shed 1.9% to 49,918. Russell 2000 was the polite exception, off just 0.4% at 2,867 - small-cap domestic companies don't import much oil, so they got to watch from the sidelines. Gold: $4,104, basically flat. That's the strangest number in the session. A stagflationary shock, a chokehold on 20% of global crude, and gold's answer was a 0.09% yawn. Either the geopolitical premium was already priced in, or gold has completely stopped being gold.

The CPI print killed the rate-cut calendar. +0.5% MoM, +4.2% YoY is not a blip. It's a regime. New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh walks into the June 16-17 FOMC not with a pivot to offer but with a live hike risk to manage. Every valuation assumption that powered the 2025 rally just got repriced in one number. Then Iran turned off the valve on 20 million barrels of daily global oil flow, Brent spiked to $94.55, and Kuwait closed its airspace. The 10-year yield barely moved - down just 2.4 bps to 4.53%. The bond market isn't calling this a flight to safety. It's calling it inflation. The pre-market bounce isn't optimism. It's short-covering.

The One Trade: Long $XLE

Iran's Hormuz closure is the most severe energy supply shock in decades. Brent spiked to $94.55. The pre-market futures bounce gives a cleaner entry than yesterday's panic open - less slippage, same thesis.

Confirms: WTI crude holds above $89 by 10:00 AM ET and XLE trades above its prior session close in the first 30 minutes. That confirms the energy bid is real and not just overnight futures noise.

Kill switch: A confirmed U.S. Navy breakthrough of the Hormuz blockade or a credible Iran ceasefire announcement - either collapses the geopolitical risk premium and reverses the oil spike within hours. Get out fast.
