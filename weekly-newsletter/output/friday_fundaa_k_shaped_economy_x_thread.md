# X Thread — Friday Fundaa: K-Shaped Economy

1/ GDP grew 2.1% last year. Wages up 3.9%. Unemployment 4.3%. The economy is doing great.

Your rent is $500 more a month. Groceries $130 more. Health insurance $120 more.

Something doesn't add up. But it's not a glitch. It's math.

2/ Your 2020 vs 2026 tab:

Rent: $1,200 → $1,700/month (+$500)
Groceries: $450 → $580/month (+$130)
Health insurance: +$120/month

Total: $750 more every month. $9,000 more every year. On things you cannot cut.

3/ Now the other side of the ledger.

$30,000 in a plain S&P 500 index fund in January 2020. No stock-picking. No trading. Just ownership.

Today: $67,000. A gain of $37,000. No salary negotiation required.

4/ GDP averaged these two realities and said: the economy is fine.

It's not wrong. GDP is a weighted average of every dollar of output. The problem: the two groups being averaged are moving in completely opposite directions.

5/ Who owns what:

Top 1%: own 31% of all US wealth and nearly half the stock market.
Bottom 50%: own 2.5% of wealth and just 1% of stocks.

When assets compound, the gains don't reach paychecks. They reach brokerage accounts.

6/ This is the K-shaped economy.

Top branch: asset owners. Going up.
Bottom branch: wage earners. Going sideways.

GDP is the average of both. The average looks fine. The branches are not in the same conversation.
