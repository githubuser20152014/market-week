Equities just had their best week in months. The Nasdaq gained 4.52%. Emerging markets surged 5.38%. The S&P 500 touched a fresh intraday record. Here's why you shouldn't fully trust it.

The ceasefire that triggered the rally lasted 72 hours. By Friday, the U.S. and Iran were back to playing chicken in the Strait of Hormuz, where 1,550 vessels from 87 countries remain stranded. The underlying risk didn't resolve. The market priced in peace and then quietly repriced it back out by the close.

---

The dissenting markets told a different story.

Bonds didn't buy it. The 10-year Treasury yield fell 4 basis points to 4.36% on the week. When equities are surging and bonds are also rallying, one of them is wrong about growth durability.

Gold refused to give up its premium. It closed at $4,720, up 3.04%, while equities also hit records. Gold and stocks rising simultaneously is not a normal risk-on rotation. Central banks bought 244 tonnes of gold in Q1. PCE inflation sits at 4.5%. The FOMC voted 8-4 to hold - the widest internal split in 30 years. Gold isn't trading fear. It's trading Fed paralysis.

Crude told the most honest story. WTI's weekly range was $88.66 to $107.46 - a $19 range in a single week. It closed down 4.32%. The UAE formally left OPEC on May 1 and took cartel pricing discipline with it. This is not a market with a resolved view on oil. It is a market that moved on every single headline out of the Persian Gulf.

---

The corporate proof point that mattered most: Palantir's revenue surged 85% to $1.63B, with U.S. commercial revenue up 133%. Alphabet gained 12% on disciplined AI spending. The enterprise AI capex cycle is real, it is accelerating, and it is driving the Nasdaq's outperformance relative to the broader market.

---

The second-order story nobody is covering: Friday's jobs report printed 115,000 jobs added in April - nearly double the 62,000 consensus. The dollar barely moved. The DXY closed the week at 97.84, down 0.21%. In a normal macro environment, a payrolls beat of that magnitude sends the dollar sharply higher as the hawk bloc reasserts itself. It didn't happen. That is the most important signal of the week: the structural dollar bull thesis is broken, and a weaker dollar is the primary fuel behind emerging markets' best week in months. If the dollar can't rally on 115,000 jobs, it is hard to see what forces the reversal.

---

Which market got it right - equities celebrating the ceasefire, or gold refusing to give up its premium?

Gold did. The ceasefire lasted 72 hours. The structural case for dollar weakness, FOMC paralysis, and EM outperformance has nothing to do with a Middle East truce. It is about a Fed that voted 8-4 to do nothing in a 4.5% PCE environment, and a dollar that shrugged off a major payrolls beat. Those conditions don't reverse on a headline.
