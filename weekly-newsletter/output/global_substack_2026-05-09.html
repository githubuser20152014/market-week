A strong payrolls beat in a 4.5% PCE environment should have sent the dollar higher. It didn't. The old playbook is broken. The market knows it.

Here's my read:

The dollar shrugged. That means the dollar bull thesis - which requires a Fed pivot to tighter policy - is structurally impaired. The FOMC is paralyzed, and the dollar is pricing it in.

You cannot hike your way out of a Hormuz blockade - rate hikes address the inflation symptom but cannot fix a supply-side energy shock. The FTSE fell -1.40%. The DAX managed +0.26%. The continent is not participating in this rally, and that gap is going to widen.
