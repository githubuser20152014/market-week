Don't let the Beijing photo op fool you.

Yesterday, Trump and Xi shook hands, pinky-promised to trade oil, and the Dow hit 50,000 for exactly five minutes. The "peace in our time" high was a beautiful hallucination - but the market's internal plumbing is telling a much darker story.

While equities celebrated the summit, the real signal was in gold and the KOSPI.

Gold dropped 2.4% ($112) in a single session. This wasn't a regime change; it was a mechanical flush of the "World War III" premium. Traders unwound their insurance because two autocrats had a nice lunch. But insurance is for people who don't trust diplomatic theater.

The KOSPI's 6.1% collapse is the market's way of saying the Taiwan "red line" didn't actually go away. Beijing was lovely, but export-heavy markets aren't buying the "resolution" narrative.

Meanwhile, the macro reality hasn't moved:
- PPI is still screaming at 6% annualized.
- Iran's missile inventory remains fully intact.
- The UAE is fast-tracking a Hormuz bypass because they know that clock is ticking.

Markets voted for the handshake yesterday. Today, they're voting on the reality of 6% inflation and regional contagion. One of them is wrong.
