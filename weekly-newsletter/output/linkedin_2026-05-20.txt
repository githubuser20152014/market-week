The bond market screamed yesterday. The 10-year hit 4.667%, a 16-month high. Equities sold off. WTI crude crashed 5.1%. Futures are green this morning.

Here's the problem: these three moves can't all be true at the same time.

Bonds are pricing in higher-for-longer. Every equity valuation model got its discount rate repriced overnight. Small caps (the canaries of domestic credit) bled 1.01% because they felt it first. That's the bond market's verdict: inflation persists, rates stay elevated, multiples compress.

But oil's 5% drop says something completely different. The Strait of Hormuz premium is deflating. The acute geopolitical fear that had been pricing in elevated energy costs? Fading. That's the oil market's verdict: the crisis shock is over, energy inflation is moderating.

The equity market, up pre-market this morning, has decided to believe both stories. That's either optimism or denial.

China just ordered 200 Boeing planes confirmed. The Pentagon tapped Shield AI for a low-cost drone program. These are real catalysts that the broader market selloff completely missed. Aerospace and defense now has structural support underneath it.

But here's what actually matters: Nvidia earnings tonight. Because the bond market and the oil market are telling opposite stories about inflation and sentiment. One of them will be proven wrong when earnings come in. A clean beat with strong guidance validates the AI narrative and suggests equities can ignore the yield repricing. A miss or soft guidance suggests the bond market was right about the discount rate resetting higher.

The market is currently betting the oil market is correct. We'll find out by 5pm ET whether that's bravery or denial.
