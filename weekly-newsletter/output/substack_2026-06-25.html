Everyone Declared AI Memory Dead for 48 Hours. Micron Posted 17% Anyway.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

Two sessions ago, Micron crashed 13% and the market decided AI infrastructure spend was vaporware. KOSPI dropped 10%. Seoul's chip complex entered a technical correction. The narrative was settled: hyperscaler capex was burning money with nothing to show for it.

Last night, Micron posted its earnings. Blowout. Margin leadership past Nvidia and Meta on AI memory demand. Pre-market: +17%. KOSPI overnight: +5.42%. The AI memory trade is back from the dead, and everyone who declared it finished has a very short memory.

Here is the problem. GDP and PCE both drop at 8:30 AM ET today. One hot inflation print and the Nasdaq futures rally, currently sitting at +2.07%, turns into a pumpkin before the open settles. The party has a fuse. Watch the clock.

Here's my read:

Yesterday's close told the story before Micron confirmed it. The Dow added 0.35% to 51,849 and the Russell gained 0.37% to 2,987 — value and small caps were not selling the AI doom narrative. The S&P dipped 0.10% to 7,358 and the Nasdaq shed 0.43% to 25,477, still paying for the prior session's chip rout. Gold sat at $3,996, parked below $4,000 with no catalyst to move it. WTI fell to $69.79, down 0.78%, as the U.S.-Iran memorandum on IAEA inspections doubled Strait of Hormuz tanker traffic in 48 hours and the market repriced the geopolitical premium out of crude. Iraq is now threatening to breach its OPEC quota anyway. Brent is under $73. The oil bulls' problem is no longer the trade war. It is a supply glut with no visible ceiling.

The cross-asset picture going into today: Nasdaq futures +2.07%, S&P futures +0.63%, with Micron's vindication as the ignition. The 10Y Treasury yield closed at 4.45% and 4.50% is the line in the sand — if PCE at 8:30 drives yields past that level, the tech bid dies mid-morning. If PCE is benign, the Micron-led re-rating has legs through noon. The ECB hiked to 2.25% into 0.8% Eurozone growth overnight. The UK's Starmer government fell, leaving a caretaker in place and GBP/USD sliding to 1.3157. Neither of those is today's story. Today's story is whether the chip trade can survive its own data.

The One Trade: Long $SOXX

Micron's 17% pre-market surge after a blowout earnings beat is the clearest single-stock-to-sector signal available today. The margin leadership over Nvidia and Meta on AI memory demand is not a Micron story. It is a sector indictment of the prior session's selling and a direct validation of the AI infrastructure build. The KOSPI's 5.42% overnight reversal confirms the re-rating is not isolated to one name.

Confirms: SOXX holds above yesterday's close and Nasdaq futures maintain their 2%+ gain through the 8:30 AM data print. If Nvidia and AMD open green and stay green past 10 AM, the rally has breadth and the position is working.

Kill switch: PCE prints above 2.8% month-over-month at 8:30 AM, driving the 10Y yield above 4.55% and reversing Nasdaq futures below +1%. That macro override kills the chip rally thesis for today — get out fast.

What else I'm watching

  • Energy (XLE, XOP): don't you dare short it without a tight stop. WTI at $69.79 reflects the Iran deal unwinding months of geopolitical premium in two days. But Iran just warned that unauthorized Strait of Hormuz transit is "unacceptable and dangerous." One tanker incident reverses this trade overnight. The quiet winner of cheap crude is industrials and transports (XLI, IYT). Nobody is pricing that in yet.
  • TLT around 8:30 is the tell. Watch it like a hawk. If TLT drops on the PCE print, yields are spiking and you trim everything growth-adjacent. If TLT holds, the rally has a clear runway. Do not pre-position in either direction before the number drops.
  • Stay away from European names today. EZU and EWU are carrying a double headwind: the ECB hiking into near-zero growth is textbook stagflation, and the UK political transition adds sterling risk on top. These are not today's trade, and the data today is not going to fix either problem.
  • Gold (GLD) is a hold, not a chase. $3,996 is consolidation, not a breakout. The Iran deal removes one safe-haven driver. A hot PCE print strengthens the dollar and pressures gold short-term. Wait for a confirmed close above $4,000 before adding.
  • The trade nobody is talking about: industrials and transports (XLI, IYT) are the structural beneficiary of WTI at $69. Lower energy input costs expand margins for every manufacturer and shipper that isn't an oil company. The rotation hasn't shown up in price yet.
