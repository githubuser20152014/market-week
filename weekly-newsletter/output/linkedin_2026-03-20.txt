New Home Sales Day: Stocks Dipped. Here's Where to Stand.

Stocks slipped. The S&P 500 closed 6,606.49 (-0.27%), the Nasdaq 22,105.36 (-0.21%), the Dow 46,021.43 (-0.44%), the Russell 2000 2,470.89 (-0.31%). Broad selloff — all four major indices closed in the red. Elsewhere: Gold fell 0.64% to $4,830.00, the 10-year Treasury yield rose 4 bps to 4.32%, WTI crude rose 1.59% to $100.22/bbl, and the dollar strengthened 0.33%.

Geopolitics ran the session yesterday. Trump invokes Pearl Harbor in front of Japanese prime minister to defend Iran attack secrecy. Middle East escalation raises the risk premium across oil, shipping, and global supply chains — even headlines that don't immediately disrupt flows move markets. Investors reprice tail risk first, ask questions later. Gold fell 0.64% alongside equities — worth flagging. When gold drops with stocks, it's usually a signal that the dollar is the real safe-haven trade. Investors went to cash and USD, not bullion.

Here's what this means for your portfolio. Dollar strength (+0.33%) is a quiet headwind for multinationals. S&P 500 large caps collect revenue abroad but report in USD — that gap eats into earnings. GLD and commodity ETFs (DJP) face the same drag. WTI above $100/bbl is a macro drag that's hard to ignore. It raises input costs across the supply chain, keeps headline CPI sticky, and makes the Fed's path to rate cuts harder to justify. Energy ETFs (XLE) are in focus — but geopolitical oil spikes can reverse fast. Don't chase.

Watch: oil — above $100/bbl it starts pressuring consumer spending and complicating the Fed's inflation story; geopolitical headlines — escalation or de-escalation will move oil, FX, and risk sentiment quickly; gold — if risk-off sentiment re-intensifies, gold could recover sharply as the safe-haven trade catches up.

Pre-market is cautious (S&P -0.18% | Nasdaq -0.20% | Dow -0.40%). Sellers are pushing again. The question is whether buyers show up — or don't.

APAC closed broadly lower overnight — ASX 200 led (-1.65%), while Nikkei 225 lagged (-3.38%).
