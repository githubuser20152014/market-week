The market just handed Japan a 4.2% week, hit new all-time highs in the Nasdaq and S&P 500, and priced in cheaper oil. The narrative is "Hormuz is opening. Risk is off. Buy equities."

Here's why I don't fully buy it.

**Bonds didn't buy it either.** The 10-year Treasury fell 9bps to 4.45% on the same week equities hit ATHs. Bonds were buying growth fear. Equities were buying all-time highs. Those are two different macro reads trading simultaneously. Historically, the bond market is not the one that apologizes first.

**Gold refused to leave.** In a risk-on week where oil fell 10.86% and equities rallied, gold should have sold off. It added 0.91% to $4,560 instead. Gold at $4,560 in a bullish week is not decoration. It is the safe-haven bid telling you the Warsh Fed uncertainty and the Middle East tail risk are not resolved.

**Crude told the whole story.** WTI had a $13 intraweek range - $86.35 to $99.43. Trump said the Hormuz deal was "largely negotiated." Then the U.S. struck southern Iran. WTI closed at $87.36. That is not a market pricing peace. That is a market pricing a pause between the last headline and the next one.

**The corporate proof point.** Micron surged 19.3% to a $1 trillion market cap on "insatiable" AI memory demand. This pulled the Nasdaq to a new ATH at 26,972 alongside the S&P 500. The AI hardware cycle is accelerating through geopolitical noise, Fed hawkishness, and energy volatility. The market is not wrong to own it. But it is pricing perfection.

**The second-order story nobody is covering: EUR/GBP.** The ECB pre-committed to a June rate hike based on 3.0% Eurozone inflation. The UK is stuck: retail sales collapsed 1.3% in April alongside 2.8% CPI - stagflation-lite. The BoE has no clean policy move. Two central banks, two diverging paths, one currency cross that has not fully repriced the gap. The Japan trade is obvious. This one is not.

**Which market got it right?**

Bonds are buying growth fear while equities buy ATHs. Gold is bid in a risk-on week. WTI swung $13 and settled nowhere clean. The answer is that nobody got it right this week because the Hormuz situation is not resolved and the Warsh Fed is an unknown quantity with a hawkish lean. Japan's 4.2% is real and structurally justified. The Nasdaq's ATH is real and AI-driven. But the safe-haven bid underneath all of it is also real. The market is running two contradictory narratives in parallel and daring the next headline to force a decision.
