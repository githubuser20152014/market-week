The Negotiations Are Underway. The Missiles Also Are. WTI: $96.65.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

Trump says negotiations with Iran are "underway." U.S. Central Command says it ran strikes on Qeshm Island. Iranian drones hit Kuwait's T1 airport. WTI crude jumped 3.08% to $96.65. The S&P 500 closed at 7,609.78, up 0.13%. These are not contradictions the market is trying to resolve. They are contradictions the market has decided to ignore.

Secretary Rubio went to the Senate Foreign Relations Committee and testified that Iran has mined "large segments" of the Strait of Hormuz. The Strait is a 21-mile chokepoint carrying roughly 20% of global oil. Right now it is not closed - it is just mined, which is the geopolitical equivalent of holding a gun sideways and calling it diplomacy.

The S&P 500 would like you to know it is up 0.13% and has no further comment.

Here's my read:

The oil shock is real and structural. WTI surged 3.08% to $96.65 with Brent testing $98, and this is not a spike to be faded - a mined strait does not un-mine itself on a Tuesday. The cross-asset read is sharper: gold fell 0.33% to $4,474.40 in the same session. When oil surges on a geopolitical shock and gold falls, the market is buying real commodity over store-of-value. That rotation means the inflation trade is front-running the Fed, not the fear trade front-running a crisis. Add two more layers: fresh tariffs on 60 economies (10-12.5% duties covering Canada, Mexico, the UK, and 57 others) and a new Fed chair whose first hire wrote the Fed chapter in Project 2025. The macro setup is $96 oil, tariff-driven import inflation, and a Fed whose institutional independence is now a legitimate question. The Russell 2000 led at +0.90% while the Nasdaq crawled +0.03% - either a broadening story or the last gasp before reality arrives. The Nikkei surged 2.50% on prior optimism. The Hang Seng fell 1.56%, which is Asia pricing the actual supply and tariff exposure. The bond market took yields down 2 bps. That is not a call. That is a shrug.

The One Trade: Long $XLE

The Strait of Hormuz is mined and WTI is at $96.65: this is a supply disruption with no near-term physical resolution, and energy equities have not yet fully priced a sustained $95-plus crude environment.

Confirms: XLE holds above its prior-day close by 10:30 AM ET and WTI futures stay above $95.50 through the morning session.

Kill switch: Trump confirms active diplomatic progress with Tehran before noon, crude drops below $93, and the geopolitical premium collapses. Get out fast.
