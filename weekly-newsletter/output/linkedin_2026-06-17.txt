The Dow hit a record 52,000 yesterday. The Nasdaq dropped 1.15% the same afternoon.

That is not a mixed signal. That is a market choosing its winners with surgical precision - and the loser is whatever was funded in cheap yen.

The Bank of Japan just ended 30 years of free money. Here is why that matters more than the Hormuz peace deal.

---

BONDS: The 10-year yield ticked up 2.4 basis points to 4.487%. Not a panic move. But the bond market is not celebrating the peace deal either. It is waiting. Watching to see whether Kevin Warsh at today's 1:00 PM FOMC statement gives it a reason to rally. The Fed holds at 3.75%, almost certainly. The question is whether Warsh says enough to validate the morning futures rip - or leaves the market guessing against a 4.49% yield ceiling.

GOLD: Gold held $4,343 even as Hormuz peace headlines were supposed to gut the safe-haven bid. It did not move. Central banks buying physical metal for reserve purposes do not care about ceasefire timelines. That is a structurally different bid than the fear trade, and it has a floor the peace narrative cannot touch.

CRUDE: WTI fell another 0.76% to $75.47 as the war premium finished unwinding. The IEA is now flagging demand destruction from the Iran conflict. The Strait of Hormuz reopens June 20. Oil is going lower, and that deflationary impulse is the single biggest gift the Fed has received in 18 months. Warsh knows this.

---

The market has not priced that moat yet.
