They Spent $200 Billion on AI Infrastructure. Micron Down 13% Is the Invoice.

The Morning Brief · Market intelligence at the open

The receipt arrived yesterday. Two years of hyperscaler AI capex announcements, data center groundbreakings, and GPU procurement headlines — and Micron's earnings handed the market an itemized bill. Micron dropped 13%. Alphabet, Amazon, and Broadcom each took multi-session hits. The question the market has been politely avoiding — does this capex produce earnings, or does it produce press releases — is getting answered, and the answer is moving in one direction.

The cross-asset read made it worse. Gold fell 1.1% to $4,085 alongside equities while the dollar climbed to 101.64, a 13-month high. Nobody reached for safety. They reached for cash. That is not a flight to safety. That is a flight. When risk-off doesn't bid gold or bonds, it means the selling is orderly and conviction-driven, not panicked. Orderly conviction is harder to reverse than panic.

This morning, Nasdaq futures are up 0.3% on no new fundamental catalyst. The KOSPI bounced 3.3% overnight on semi short-covering, which will lift SMH at the open and make the whole session feel like recovery. It is not recovery until Nasdaq reclaims 25,587 by 11am ET. Until then, every pre-market green number is a favor — and favors get taken back.

Here's my read:

This is a two-session drawdown with conviction. The Nasdaq fell 2.2% to 25,587 yesterday, following Monday's 351-point drop. Sellers are not panicking — they are being methodical. The driver is AI capex skepticism, not a Fed surprise or macro data shock: Micron's 13% collapse on earnings, paired with multi-session selloffs in Alphabet, Amazon, and Broadcom, is the market reassessing whether hyperscaler infrastructure spending translates into near-term profitability. Investors funded a construction boom and are now asking where the rent checks are. The S&P 500 dropped 1.4% to 7,365 and the Russell 2000 lost 1% to 2,975 — small-caps confirming, not just suffering. The dollar's 13-month high at 101.64 is the tell: when global tech portfolios de-risk, dollar-denominated cash is the destination of choice. Gold and bonds did not get the memo, because there was no macro panic to receive it.

The One Trade: Long $SQQQ

Nasdaq futures are up 0.3% on no new fundamental catalyst, making the pre-market bounce a sell into, not a chase. The AI capex skepticism that drove a two-day drawdown is fully unresolved heading into PCE data later this week. A dead-cat bounce on no news is still a dead cat.

Confirms: Nasdaq cash index fails to reclaim 25,587 by 11am ET and fades toward 25,300 on above-average volume, confirming sellers are still in control after the open gap.

Kill switch: Nasdaq cash closes above 25,800 on volume, or a named hyperscaler (Alphabet, Microsoft, Amazon) issues a positive AI monetization update that directly addresses capex ROI concerns. If that happens, get out fast — the thesis inverts.

  • $QQQ and $SMH — reduce into the gap-up open, don't you dare chase it. The bounce has no catalyst. The KOSPI short-covering will flatter SMH intraday; watch whether it holds above yesterday's close on volume by midday. Without volume confirmation, fade the move.

  • Nasdaq 25,587 — the number that settles the session. Reclaim it on volume by 11am and the bounce is real. Fail to reclaim it and sellers never left. Watch nothing else until that question is answered.
