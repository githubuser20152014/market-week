## Email / Subscriber version

**Subject: Finally — a place to make sense of what the market is actually doing**

If you've ever read a financial headline and thought *"I know those words individually..."* — this is for you.

We just rebuilt **frameworkfoundry.info**, and the new version has something we've been wanting to build for a long time: a proper starting point for people who are serious about understanding markets, but didn't go to business school.

Here's what's new:

**Market IQ — 36 plain-English explainers**
CPI. NFP. The yield curve. Fed funds rate. PCE. These terms show up in the news every week, but nobody ever explains what they actually *mean* — or why you should care. Market IQ does exactly that. Each card breaks down one indicator in plain English: what it measures, what the current reading is, and what it means for your money. Search it, filter by category, or browse A–Z. Bookmark it. You'll use it more than you think.

**Friday Fundaa — one idea, clearly explained**
Every Friday, we take one economic concept that's in the news and explain it properly. Not a tweet. Not a 45-minute podcast. Just a clear, focused read that leaves you with a real understanding. The first one covers shrinkflation — why your grocery bill feels wrong even when the price tag hasn't changed.

**A homepage that actually makes sense**
Weekly markets coverage, daily briefings, Market IQ, and Friday Fundaa each have their own tab. No hunting around. No information overload.

**Coming soon — two more sections we're building out:**

*Investing* — A step-by-step guide to building your first investment portfolio from scratch. Which accounts to open, how to think about asset allocation, what "diversified" actually means in practice, and how to start without feeling like you need to know everything first. Built for people who've been meaning to start investing seriously but kept putting it off.

*Expat Investing* — For Americans who are exploring what life looks like abroad — whether that's a slow move, a part-time base, or just keeping options open — without walking away from their US financial life. Tax implications, brokerage accounts that work across borders, how to think about currency exposure, and the financial questions nobody warns you about before you go. If you've been watching the world and wondering what a life in two countries actually costs, this section is for you.

Both are in progress. Subscribers hear about new content first.

And if you want all of this in your inbox — it's free. Two publications, no noise:

- **Market Day Break** — Every weekday morning, before 8 am: what moved overnight, what to watch today, and what it means. Two minutes before the market opens.
- **Framework Foundry Weekly** — Every Saturday: the full week in markets, with macro context and positioning notes for ETF investors.

The market doesn't wait for you to catch up. But it does become a lot less intimidating once you know what you're looking at.
