**Issue #1: The one decision that controls 90% of your returns**

Most people with a retirement account have never really looked at how it's set up. There's a number on a statement. They know it exists. But the actual allocation — how much is in stocks, how much is in bonds — was set by a default enrollment years ago and never revisited.

A 1986 study tracked 91 large pension funds for a decade and found that 91.5% of long-term return variability came down to a single decision. Not which funds the managers picked. Not when they bought or sold. How much they had in stocks versus bonds versus cash.

Issue #1 covers what that means in practice: why it matters more at 48 than it did at 28, what the gap between a 70/30 and a 40/60 portfolio actually looks like in dollar terms over 17 years, and one action to take this week.
