The Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed, Brent crude is screaming at $126, and everyone is crying over a $4.11 gallon of gas. 

That’s cute. But your commute is just the appetizer. 

While the news cycle obsesses over naval demining and high-stakes talks in Islamabad, your credit card is screaming for a different reason. The visible pain at the pump is just the tip of the energy iceberg. 

Here is the math your wallet didn’t ask for, but is paying anyway.

### ⛽ **The Decoy: The $700 Commute Tax**
Before the conflict, US gasoline averaged roughly $3.21 per gallon. Today, it’s $4.11. That’s a 90-cent jump that you see every single time you drive past a Shell station.

If you drive a typical vehicle and fill up 15 gallons a week, that’s an extra $13.50 every time you hit the pump. Over a year, that’s **$702 directly out of your pocket** just to get to work. It’s the visible wound—annoying, but survivable. The real damage is quiet.

### 🛒 **The Silent Assassin: The $150 Logistics Tax**
Diesel spikes don’t just vanish. They flow into everything that arrives on a truck—which is basically everything you own. 

Retailers are currently passing through 100% of these fuel surcharges to maintain their razor-thin margins. If you spend $150 a week on groceries, and just 2% of that cost is the "fuel pass-through," that’s another **$150 per year** added to your receipt. If you aren't factoring this in, you aren't paying attention.

### ✈️ **The Extortion: The $300 Vacation Penalty**
Jet fuel is up 95% since February 28. Airlines have already preemptively slashed 20,000 flights from their summer schedules. 

For the flights that *are* still running, you’re seeing it in "fuel surcharges" that make a $400 domestic flight suddenly cost $550. If your family takes just one major trip this year, that’s another **$300 "geopolitical premium"** bolted onto your vacation.

---

### **The Mechanism: Why you’re paying for a war across the world**
Why does a chokehold in the Persian Gulf hit your wallet in Atlanta? Because oil is a global cartel that’s currently fracturing in real-time. 

A supply shock in the Persian Gulf doesn't just raise prices for people in Dubai; it raises the price of every barrel of Brent and WTI crude on the planet. Even in the US, refiners have to pay the global market price. If they don't, they lose their supply to someone else who will. 

The "Peace Dividend" we enjoyed for decades was built on the assumption of free navigation through these chokepoints. Right now, that assumption is dead, and we’re all paying the insurance premium.

### **The Vibe**
We're all rich on paper (S&P 7200!), but we can't afford to drive to the bank to cash out. The "Hormuz Premium" is now institutionalized into your portfolio.

Now you know.
