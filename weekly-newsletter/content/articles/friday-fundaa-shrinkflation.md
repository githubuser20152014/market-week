## LinkedIn

**Your chocolate bar is lying to you.**

Same price. Same wrapper. Fewer chips.

That's shrinkflation — the sneaky cousin of inflation that nobody talks about because it's technically *not* inflation. Prices didn't go up. You just got... less.

Companies love it. Consumers notice a price hike immediately. A slightly lighter bag of chips? You shrug and think "felt like more last time."

It spiked hard in 2022-23 as input costs rose. Toilet paper rolls got narrower. OJ cartons shrank from 64oz to 52oz. A "family size" Doritos bag quietly lost 20% of its contents.

The kicker: official CPI doesn't fully capture this. It tracks price per unit — but when the unit quietly shrinks, the data looks tame while your grocery bill keeps climbing. Analysts call it **quality-adjusted inflation**. It's why "inflation is cooling" and "everything still feels expensive" can both be true at the same time.

Next time something tastes like there used to be more of it — there probably was.
