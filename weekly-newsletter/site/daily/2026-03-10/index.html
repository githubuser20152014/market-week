The Brief

Stocks edged higher. The S&P 500 closed 6,795.99 (+0.83%), the Nasdaq 22,695.95 (+1.38%), the Dow 47,740.80 (+0.50%), the Russell 2000 2,553.67 (+1.12%). Broad-based. All four major indices closed green. Elsewhere: Gold climbed 1.95% to $5,190.80, the 10-year Treasury yield held flat at 4.14%, WTI crude fell 6.86% to $88.27/bbl, and the dollar weakened 0.50%.

Geopolitics ran the session yesterday. Oil extends slide as investors assess Trump comments on Iran war, Strait of Hormuz. The Strait of Hormuz is the world's most important oil chokepoint — roughly 20% of global oil supply passes through it daily. Any credible threat moves energy markets. That ripples into inflation expectations, transportation costs, and EM currencies.

What it means for you

Here's what this means for your portfolio. Dollar weakness (-0.50%) is a tailwind for international equities and commodities. EM-focused funds (EEM, VWO) and GLD tend to benefit when the dollar softens. Oil's 6.86% decline is a quiet tailwind for consumer-facing sectors. Lower energy costs flow into margins for transportation (XTN), retail (XRT), and airlines.

Watch: geopolitical headlines — escalation or de-escalation will move oil, FX, and risk sentiment quickly.

Futures are mixed (S&P -0.01% | Nasdaq +0.08% | Dow +0.00%). No conviction either way. The first 30 minutes will be more informative than anything said before the bell.

APAC closed broadly higher overnight — KOSPI led (+5.35%), while Nifty 50 lagged (+0.97%).
